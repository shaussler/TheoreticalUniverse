Differential Operators#

by St√©phane Haussler

In this article, I use the language of differential forms to systematically express the derivative, the differential, the gradient, the divergence, the curl and the laplacian in 3-dimensional Euclidean space.

I assume the reader possesses a strong grasp of vector calculus, a working understanding of differential forms, the exterior product, musical operators, as well as the Hoddge dual. To learn about differential forms, see yet another great video serie by Michael Penn. Implicitely assumed with the above requisites is a basic understanding of tensor calculus, and in particular the concept of vector and covector. Some familiarity with the concepts of of either Grassman Algebra, Clifford Algebra (AKA Geometric Algebra), or Lie Algebra is not necessary but certainly welcome for a deeper understanding of the antisymmetries at hand.

Derivative#

The partial derivatives are the basis vectors. I am personally still wrapping my head around it and refer for details to Tangent Space Basis and Constructing Tangent Vectors by qncubed3, or Coordinate Basis by brightsideofmaths.

Proposition

\[\mathbf{e}_i = \partial_i\]

Differential#

The differential applied to the partial derivative results in:

\[dx^i вИВ_j = ќі^i_j\]

This is the definition for a covector, and we conclude that the differential operator is a covector.

Proposition

\[\mathbf{e}^i = dx^i\]

Gradient#

Proposition

\[(df)^{вЩѓ} = \mathbf{вИЗ} f\]

Expand \(df\):

\[(df)^вЩѓ = ( dx вИВ_x f + dy вИВ_y f + dz вИВ_z f)^вЩѓ\]

Distribute the sharp operator \(вЩѓ\):

\[(df)^вЩѓ = (dx)^вЩѓ вИВ_x f + (dy)^вЩѓ вИВ_y f + (dz)^вЩѓ вИВ_z f\]

Which permits to conclude:

\[(df)^вЩѓ = вИВ_x f вИВ_x + вИВ_y f вИВ_y + вИВ_z f вИВ_z\]

In other words and for clarity:

\[(df)^вЩѓ = вИВ_x f \mathbf{e}_x + вИВ_y f \mathbf{e}_y + вИВ_z f \mathbf{e}_z\]

Divergence#

Proposition

\[вЛЖ d вЛЖ F^вЩ≠ = \mathbf{вИЗ} \cdot \mathbf{F}\]

We begin with \(F\) as a vector \(\mathbf{F} = F^\sharp = F^i \partial_i\) and flatten:

\[\begin{split}F^вЩ≠ = \begin{bmatrix} F^x вИВ_x \\ F^y вИВ_y \\ F^z вИВ_z \\ \end{bmatrix}^вЩ≠ = \begin{bmatrix} F^x (вИВ_x)^вЩ≠ \\ F^y (вИВ_y)^вЩ≠ \\ F^z (вИВ_z)^вЩ≠ \\ \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} F^x dx \\ F^y dy \\ F^z dz \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Apply the \(\star\) operator:

\[\begin{split}вЛЖ F^вЩ≠ = вЛЖ \begin{bmatrix} F^x dx \\ F^y dy \\ F^z dz \\ \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} F^x вЛЖ dx \\ F^y вЛЖ dy \\ F^z вЛЖ dz \\ \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} F^x dy вИІ dz \\ F^y dz вИІ dx \\ F^z dx вИІ dy \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Apply the \(d\) operator:

\[\begin{split}d вЛЖ F^вЩ≠ = d \begin{bmatrix} F^x dy вИІ dz \\ F^y dz вИІ dx \\ F^z dx вИІ dy \\ \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} вИВ_x F^x dx вИІ dy вИІ dz \\ вИВ_y F^y dy вИІ dz вИІ dx \\ вИВ_z F^z dz вИІ dx вИІ dy \\ \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} вИВ_x F^x dx вИІ dy вИІ dz \\ вИВ_y F^y dx вИІ dy вИІ dz \\ вИВ_z F^z dx вИІ dy вИІ dz \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Which can be brought back to a zero form by applying yet again the Hodge star \(вЛЖ\):

\[\begin{split}вЛЖ d вЛЖ F^вЩ≠ = \begin{bmatrix} вИВ_x F^x вЛЖ dx вИІ dy вИІ dz \\ вИВ_y F^y вЛЖ dx вИІ dy вИІ dz \\ вИВ_z F^z вЛЖ dx вИІ dy вИІ dz \\ \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} вИВ_x F^x \mathbf{1} \\ вИВ_y F^y \mathbf{1} \\ вИВ_z F^z \mathbf{1} \\ \end{bmatrix} = вИВ_x F^x + вИВ_y F^y + вИВ_z F^z\end{split}\]

Curl#

Proposition

\[(вЛЖ(dF^вЩ≠))^вЩѓ = вИЗ^вЩѓ в®ѓ F^вЩѓ\]

The vector field is:

\[\begin{split}F^вЩѓ = \begin{bmatrix} F^x вИВ_x \\ F^y вИВ_y \\ F^z вИВ_z \\ \end{bmatrix} = F^x вИВ_x + F^y вИВ_y + F^z вИВ_z\end{split}\]

Flattening the vector field result in:

\[\begin{split}F^\flat = \begin{bmatrix} F^x \; dx \\ F^y \; dy \\ F^z \; dz \\ \end{bmatrix} = F^x dx + F^y dy + F^z dz\end{split}\]

Taking the differential, we have:

\[\begin{split}dF^вЩ≠ = \begin{bmatrix} вИВ_x F^x dx вИІ dx & вИВ_y F^x dy вИІ dx & вИВ_z F^x dz вИІ dx \\ вИВ_x F^y dx вИІ dy & вИВ_y F^y dy вИІ dy & вИВ_z F^y dz вИІ dy \\ вИВ_x F^z dx вИІ dz & вИВ_y F^z dy вИІ dy & вИВ_z F^z dz вИІ dz \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Or with more natural row/column convention:

\[\begin{split}dF^вЩ≠ = \begin{bmatrix} вИВ_x F^x dx вИІ dx & вИВ_x F^y dx вИІ dy & вИВ_x F^z dx вИІ dz \\ вИВ_y F^x dy вИІ dx & вИВ_y F^y dy вИІ dy & вИВ_y F^z dy вИІ dy \\ вИВ_z F^x dz вИІ dx & вИВ_z F^y dz вИІ dy & вИВ_z F^z dz вИІ dz \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Where \(dx^i вИІ dx^i = 0\):

\[\begin{split}dF^вЩ≠ = \begin{bmatrix} & вИВ_x F^y dx вИІ dy & вИВ_x F^z dx вИІ dz \\ вИВ_y F^x dy вИІ dx & & вИВ_y F^z dy вИІ dy \\ вИВ_z F^x dz вИІ dx & вИВ_z F^y dz вИІ dy & \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

And \(dx^i вИІ dx^j = -dx^j вИІ dx^i\):

\[\begin{split}dF^вЩ≠ = \begin{bmatrix} & + вИВ_x F^y dx вИІ dy & - вИВ_x F^z dz вИІ dx \\ - вИВ_y F^x dx вИІ dy & & + вИВ_y F^z dy вИІ dy \\ + вИВ_z F^x dz вИІ dx & - вИВ_z F^y dy вИІ dz & \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

That we reorder to:

\[\begin{split}dF^вЩ≠ = \begin{bmatrix} + вИВ_y F^z dy вИІ dy - вИВ_z F^y \; dy вИІ dz \\ + вИВ_z F^x dz вИІ dx - вИВ_x F^z \; dz вИІ dx \\ + вИВ_x F^y dx вИІ dy - вИВ_y F^x \; dx вИІ dy \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]
\[\begin{split}dF^вЩ≠ = \begin{bmatrix} (вИВ_y F^z - вИВ_z F^y) \; dy вИІ dz \\ (вИВ_z F^x - вИВ_x F^z) \; dz вИІ dx \\ (вИВ_x F^y - вИВ_y F^x) \; dx вИІ dy \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Where we can now take the star operator:

\[\begin{split}вЛЖ dF^вЩ≠ = \begin{bmatrix} (вИВ_y F^z - вИВ_z F^y) вЛЖ dy вИІ dz \\ (вИВ_z F^x - вИВ_x F^z) вЛЖ dz вИІ dx \\ (вИВ_x F^y - вИВ_y F^x) вЛЖ dx вИІ dy \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]
\[\begin{split}вЛЖ dF^вЩ≠ = \begin{bmatrix} (вИВ_y F^z - вИВ_z F^y) \; dx \\ (вИВ_z F^x - вИВ_x F^z) \; dy \\ (вИВ_x F^y - вИВ_y F^x) \; dz \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

We can then sharpen the covector to its vector form:

\[\begin{split}(вЛЖ dF^вЩ≠)^вЩѓ = \begin{bmatrix} (вИВ_y F^z - вИВ_z F^y) \; dx^вЩѓ \\ (вИВ_z F^x - вИВ_x F^z) \; dy^вЩѓ \\ (вИВ_x F^y - вИВ_y F^x) \; dz^вЩѓ \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]
\[\begin{split}(вЛЖ dF^вЩ≠)^вЩѓ = \begin{bmatrix} (вИВ_y F^z - вИВ_z F^y) \; вИВ_x \\ (вИВ_z F^x - вИВ_x F^z) \; вИВ_y \\ (вИВ_x F^y - вИВ_y F^x) \; вИВ_t \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Where we have recovered the expression of the curl of a vector field:

\[\begin{split}вИЗ^вЩѓ в®ѓ F^вЩѓ = \begin{bmatrix} (вИВ_y F^z - вИВ F^y) \; вИВ_x \\ (вИВ_z F^x - вИВ F^z) \; вИВ_y \\ (вИВ_x F^y - вИВ F^x) \; вИВ_z \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Laplacian#

Proposition

\[вЛЖ d вЛЖ d f = \mathbf{вИЗ}^2 f\]

The differential of a function (zero form) is:

\[df = вИВ_x f dx + вИВ_y f dy + вИВ_z f dz\]

Taking the Hodge dual:

\[вЛЖ df = вИВ_x f dy вИІ dz + вИВ_y dz вИІ dx + вИВ_z f dx вИІ dy\]

Taking the differential

\[\begin{split}d вЛЖ df &= \frac{вИВ^2 f}{вИВ x^2} dx вИІ dy вИІ dz + \frac{вИВ^2 f}{вИВ y^2} dy вИІ dz вИІ dx + \frac{вИВ^2 f}{вИВ z^2} dz вИІ dx вИІ dy \\ &= \frac{вИВ^2 f}{вИВ x^2} dx вИІ dy вИІ dz + \frac{вИВ^2 f}{вИВ y^2} dx вИІ dy вИІ dz + \frac{вИВ^2 f}{вИВ z^2} dx вИІ dy вИІ dz \\ &= \left( \frac{вИВ^2 f}{вИВ x^2} + \frac{вИВ^2 f}{вИВ y^2} + \frac{вИВ^2 f}{вИВ z^2} \right) \; dx вИІ dy вИІ dz \\\end{split}\]

Taking the Hodge dual, we tranform volumes to functions and obtain the expression for the laplacian:

\[вЛЖ d вЛЖ df = \left( \frac{вИВ^2 f}{вИВ x^2} + \frac{вИВ^2 f}{вИВ y^2} + \frac{вИВ^2 f}{вИВ z^2} \right)\]

The Laplacian in vector calculus is the divergence \(\mathbf{вИЗ} \cdot\) applied to the gradiant of a function \(\mathbf{вИЗ} f\):

\[\mathbf{вИЗ} \cdot \mathbf{вИЗ} f\]

Where the divergence is \(вЛЖ d вЛЖ\) and the gradient of a function is \(df\) brings the zero-form to a zero form.

The Laplace-De Rham Operator#

Warning

Under construction

The Laplacian is only valid for functions (zero-forms). The Laplacian can be generalized to n-forms with the Laplace-de Rham operator.

\[вИЖ = dќі + ќіd = (d + ќі)^2\]

Considering a k-form in a space with dimension \(n\) and parity s, the general expression for codifferential is:

\[ќі = (-1)^{n(k+1)+1} s вЛЖ d вЛЖ\]

In Euclidean space with \(n=3\), the metric signature is \((+,+,+)\) and the parity therefore \((1)в®ѓ(1)в®ѓ(1)=1\). The codifferential is then:

\[\delta = (-1)^k вЛЖ d вЛЖ\]

Laplace-De Rham Operator in Euclidean Space

\[dќі + ќіd = d вЛЖ d вЛЖ + (-1)^k вЛЖ d вЛЖ d\]

In Minkowski space with \(n=4\), the metric signature is \((+,-,-,-)\) and the parity therefore \((+1)в®ѓ(-1)в®ѓ(-1)в®ѓ(-1)=-1\). The codifferential is then:

\[\begin{split}ќі & = (-1)^{n(k+1)+1} s вЛЖ d вЛЖ \\ & = (-1)^{4(k+1)+1} (-1) вЛЖ d вЛЖ \\ & = (-1)^{4(k+1)+2} вЛЖ d вЛЖ \\ & = вЛЖ d вЛЖ \\\end{split}\]

Laplace-De Rham Operator in Minkowski Space

\[dќі + ќіd = d вЛЖ d вЛЖ + вЛЖ d вЛЖ d\]
