The Exterior Derivative of Rotations in Spacetime#

by Stéphane Haussler

This article investigates the exterior derivative of rotations expressed in differential form. We employ the Cartan-Hodge formalism within the context of Minkowski spacetime. Following the systematic calculations presented in this article, I demonstrate in Of Maxwell Equations and Rotations that a twist in spacetime leads to the equations governing electromagnetism.

While the concept might not be entirely novel, I have not yet encountered some of my observations elsewhere. If you are aware of relevant references, feel free to open an issue and I will include them. I you identify any errors, you can either open an issue, or directly submit corrections via a merge request to my GitHub repository: Theoretical Universe.

Spacetime Rotations#

Rotations in spacetime can occur in six independent planes. Any rotation can be decomposed into a linear combination of basis rotations within each plane:

Rotation in Minkowski Space

\[\begin{split}R^{♯♯} = \begin{bmatrix} a \; ∂_t ∧ ∂_x \\ b \; ∂_t ∧ ∂_y \\ c \; ∂_t ∧ ∂_z \\ d \; ∂_y ∧ ∂_z \\ e \; ∂_z ∧ ∂_x \\ f \; ∂_x ∧ ∂_y \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Spacetime Rotations in Differential Forms#

The generic rotation above is doubly contravariant, given in terms of the wedge product \(∧\) of vectors corresponding to our physical understanding of space (and time). By fully flattening, we obtain the associated doubly covariant differential 2-form representative of the rotation:

Rotation in Differential Form

\[\begin{split}R^{♭♭} = \left[ \begin{aligned} - & a \; dt ∧ dx \\ - & b \; dt ∧ dy \\ - & c \; dt ∧ dz \\ & d \; dy ∧ dz \\ & e \; dz ∧ dx \\ & f \; dx ∧ dy \\ \end{aligned} \right]\end{split}\]

All Calculation Steps

Apply the flat operator \(flat\) to each components of the doubly contravariant rotation tensor \(R^{♯♯}\):

\[R^{♭♭} = (R^{♯♯})^{♭♭}\]

Expand the full expression and distribute the flat operators \(♭\):

\[\begin{split}R^{♭♭} = \begin{bmatrix} a \; ∂_t^♭ ∧ ∂_x^♭ \\ b \; ∂_t^♭ ∧ ∂_y^♭ \\ c \; ∂_t^♭ ∧ ∂_z^♭ \\ d \; ∂_y^♭ ∧ ∂_z^♭ \\ e \; ∂_z^♭ ∧ ∂_x^♭ \\ f \; ∂_x^♭ ∧ ∂_y^♭ \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Expand with the Minkowski metric:

\[\begin{split}R^{♭♭} = \begin{bmatrix} a \; η_{αt} \; dx^α ∧ η_{βx} \; dx^β \\ b \; η_{αt} \; dx^α ∧ η_{βy} \; dx^β \\ c \; η_{αt} \; dx^α ∧ η_{βz} \; dx^β \\ d \; η_{αy} \; dx^α ∧ η_{βz} \; dx^β \\ e \; η_{αz} \; dx^α ∧ η_{βx} \; dx^β \\ f \; η_{αx} \; dx^α ∧ η_{βy} \; dx^β \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

The wedge product \(∧\) is bilinear and the Minkowski metric components \(η\)’s can be taken in front of the expression:

\[\begin{split}R^{♭♭} = \begin{bmatrix} a \; η_{αt} η_{βx} \; dx^α ∧ dx^β \\ b \; η_{αt} η_{βy} \; dx^α ∧ dx^β \\ c \; η_{αt} η_{βz} \; dx^α ∧ dx^β \\ d \; η_{αy} η_{βz} \; dx^α ∧ dx^β \\ e \; η_{αz} η_{βx} \; dx^α ∧ dx^β \\ f \; η_{αx} η_{βy} \; dx^α ∧ dx^β \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Identify the non-zero components of the Minkowski metric \(η_{μν}\):

\[\begin{split}R^{♭♭} = \begin{bmatrix} a \; η_{tt} η_{xx} \; dx^t ∧ dx^x \\ b \; η_{tt} η_{yy} \; dx^t ∧ dx^y \\ c \; η_{tt} η_{zz} \; dx^t ∧ dx^z \\ d \; η_{yy} η_{zz} \; dx^y ∧ dx^z \\ e \; η_{zz} η_{xx} \; dx^z ∧ dx^x \\ f \; η_{xx} η_{yy} \; dx^x ∧ dx^y \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

For readability, replace the \(dx^μ\) symbols by their explicit expressions:

\[\begin{split}dx^t &= dt \\ dx^x &= dx \\ dx^y &= dy \\ dx^z &= dz\end{split}\]

We obtain:

\[\begin{split}R^{♭♭} = \begin{bmatrix} a \; η_{tt} η_{xx} \; dt ∧ dx \\ b \; η_{tt} η_{yy} \; dt ∧ dy \\ c \; η_{tt} η_{zz} \; dt ∧ dz \\ d \; η_{yy} η_{zz} \; dy ∧ dz \\ e \; η_{zz} η_{xx} \; dz ∧ dx \\ f \; η_{xx} η_{yy} \; dx ∧ dy \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Apply the numerical values of the Minkowski metric components \(η_{μν}\):

\[\begin{split}R^{♭♭} = \begin{bmatrix} a \; (+1) (-1) \; dt ∧ dx \\ b \; (+1) (-1) \; dt ∧ dy \\ c \; (+1) (-1) \; dt ∧ dz \\ d \; (-1) (-1) \; dy ∧ dz \\ e \; (-1) (-1) \; dz ∧ dx \\ f \; (-1) (-1) \; dx ∧ dy \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Conclude:

\[\begin{split}R^{♭♭} = \left[ \begin{aligned} - & a \; dt ∧ dx \\ - & b \; dt ∧ dy \\ - & c \; dt ∧ dz \\ & d \; dy ∧ dz \\ & e \; dz ∧ dx \\ & f \; dx ∧ dy \\ \end{aligned} \right]\end{split}\]

The hodge dual is:

Hodge Dual of a Rotation in Differential Form

\[\begin{split}⋆ R^{♭♭} = \begin{bmatrix} a \; dy ∧ dz \\ b \; dz ∧ dx \\ c \; dx ∧ dy \\ d \; dt ∧ dx \\ e \; dt ∧ dy \\ f \; dt ∧ dz \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Calculation steps

Take the Hodge dual

\[\begin{split}⋆ R^{♭♭} = ⋆ \left[ \begin{aligned} - a \; dt ∧ dx \\ - b \; dt ∧ dy \\ - c \; dt ∧ dz \\ d \; dy ∧ dz \\ e \; dz ∧ dx \\ f \; dx ∧ dy \\ \end{aligned} \right]\end{split}\]

Distribute the Hodge operator

\[\begin{split}⋆ R^{♭♭} = \left[ \begin{aligned} - a \; ⋆ (dt ∧ dx) \\ - b \; ⋆ (dt ∧ dy) \\ - c \; ⋆ (dt ∧ dz) \\ d \; ⋆ (dy ∧ dz) \\ e \; ⋆ (dz ∧ dx) \\ f \; ⋆ (dx ∧ dy) \\ \end{aligned} \right]\end{split}\]

Apply Hodge dual to each basis elements

\[\begin{split}⋆ R^{♭♭} = \left[ \begin{aligned} - a \; (-1) \; dy ∧ dz \\ - b \; (-1) \; dz ∧ dx \\ - c \; (-1) \; dx ∧ dy \\ d \; (+1) \; dt ∧ dx \\ e \; (+1) \; dt ∧ dy \\ f \; (+1) \; dt ∧ dz \\ \end{aligned} \right]\end{split}\]

Conclude

\[\begin{split}⋆ R^{♭♭} = \begin{bmatrix} a \; dy ∧ dz \\ b \; dz ∧ dx \\ c \; dx ∧ dy \\ d \; dt ∧ dx \\ e \; dt ∧ dy \\ f \; dt ∧ dz \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Exterior Derivative of a Rotation#

Applying in sequence the exterior derivative operator \(d\) and the Hodge dual operator \(⋆\) to the doubly covariant rotation \(⋆ d R^{♭♭}\), we obtain:

Hodge Dual of the Exterior Derivative of Rotations in Differential Form

\[\begin{split}⋆ (dR^{♭♭}) = \left[ \begin{alignedat}{5} (& & - ∂_x d & - ∂_y e & - ∂_z f &\:) \; dt \\ (& - ∂_t d & & - ∂_y c & + ∂_z b &\:) \; dx \\ (& - ∂_t e & + ∂_x c & & - ∂_z a &\:) \; dy \\ (& - ∂_t f & - ∂_x b & + ∂_y a & &\:) \; dz \\ \end{alignedat} \right]\end{split}\]

Calculation steps

Distribute the exterior derivative

\[\begin{split}dR^{♭♭} = \begin{bmatrix} d( - a \; dt ∧ dx ) \\ d( - b \; dt ∧ dy ) \\ d( - c \; dt ∧ dz ) \\ d( + d \; dy ∧ dz ) \\ d( + e \; dz ∧ dx ) \\ d( + f \; dx ∧ dy ) \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Apply the exterior derivative

\[\begin{split}dR^{♭♭} = \begin{bmatrix} & & ∂_y (-a)\; dy ∧ dt ∧ dx & ∂_z (-a)\; dz ∧ dt ∧ dx \\ & ∂_x (-b)\; dx ∧ dt ∧ dy & & ∂_z (-b)\; dz ∧ dt ∧ dy \\ & ∂_x (-c)\; dx ∧ dt ∧ dz & ∂_y (-c)\; dy ∧ dt ∧ dz & \\ ∂_t (+d)\; dt ∧ dy ∧ dz & ∂_x (+d)\; dx ∧ dy ∧ dz & & \\ ∂_t (+e)\; dt ∧ dz ∧ dx & & ∂_y (+e)\; dy ∧ dz ∧ dx & \\ ∂_t (+f)\; dt ∧ dx ∧ dy & & & ∂_z (+f)\; dz ∧ dx ∧ dy \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Reorder exterior products, flipping the sign for every odd permutations

\[\begin{split}dR^{♭♭} = \begin{bmatrix} & & ∂_y (-a)(+1)\; dt∧dx∧dy & ∂_z (-a)(-1)\; dt∧dz∧dx \\ & ∂_x (-b)(-1)\; dt∧dx∧dy & & ∂_z (-b)(+1)\; dt∧dy∧dz \\ & ∂_x (-c)(+1)\; dt∧dz∧dx & ∂_y (-c)(-1)\; dt∧dy∧dz & \\ ∂_t (+d)(+1)\; dt∧dy∧ dz & ∂_x (+d)(+1)\; dx∧dy∧dz & & \\ ∂_t (+e)(+1)\; dt∧dz∧ dx & & ∂_y (+e)(+1)\; dx∧dy∧dz & \\ ∂_t (+f)(+1)\; dt∧dx∧ dy & & & ∂_z (+f)(+1)\; dx∧dy∧dz \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Simplify

\[\begin{split}dR^{♭♭} = \begin{bmatrix} & & ∂_y (-a)\; dt ∧ dx ∧ dy & ∂_z (+a)\; dt ∧ dz ∧ dx \\ & ∂_x (+b)\; dt ∧ dx ∧ dy & & ∂_z (-b)\; dt ∧ dy ∧ dz \\ & ∂_x (-c)\; dt ∧ dz ∧ dx & ∂_y (+c)\; dt ∧ dy ∧ dz & \\ ∂_t (+d)\; dt ∧ dy∧ dz & ∂_x (+d)\; dx ∧ dy ∧ dz & & \\ ∂_t (+e)\; dt ∧ dz∧ dx & & ∂_y (+e)\; dx ∧ dy ∧ dz & \\ ∂_t (+f)\; dt ∧ dx∧ dy & & & ∂_z (+f)\; dx ∧ dy ∧ dz \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Organize the terms into a single column

Although the specific ordering is not mandatory. However, for clarity, the free matrix representation allows us to arrange the terms in a logical manner. Note that:

  • The first row excludes the terms with \(dt\)

  • The second row excludes the terms with \(dx\)

  • The third row excludes the terms with \(dy\)

  • The fourth row excludes the terms with \(dz\)

\[\begin{split}dR = \left[ \begin{alignedat}{5} (& & + ∂_x d & + ∂_y e & + ∂_z f &\:) \; dx^x ∧ dx^y ∧ dx^z \\ (& + ∂_t d & & + ∂_y c & - ∂_z b &\:) \; dx^t ∧ dx^y ∧ dx^z \\ (& + ∂_t e & - ∂_x c & & + ∂_z a &\:) \; dx^t ∧ dx^z ∧ dx^x \\ (& + ∂_t f & + ∂_x b & - ∂_y a & &\:) \; dx^t ∧ dx^x ∧ dx^y \\ \end{alignedat} \right]\end{split}\]

Apply the Hodge dual, thereby converting 3-forms to 1-forms:

\[\begin{split}⋆(dR) = \left[ \begin{alignedat}{5} (& & + ∂_x d & + ∂_y e & + ∂_z f &\:) \; (-dt) \\ (& + ∂_t d & & + ∂_y c & - ∂_z b &\:) \; (-dx) \\ (& + ∂_t e & - ∂_x c & & + ∂_z a &\:) \; (-dy) \\ (& + ∂_t f & + ∂_x b & - ∂_y a & &\:) \; (-dz) \\ \end{alignedat} \right]\end{split}\]

Conclude

\[\begin{split}⋆ (dR) = \left[ \begin{alignedat}{5} (& & - ∂_x d & - ∂_y e & - ∂_z f &\:) \; dt \\ (& - ∂_t d & & - ∂_y c & + ∂_z b &\:) \; dx \\ (& - ∂_t e & + ∂_x c & & - ∂_z a &\:) \; dy \\ (& - ∂_t f & - ∂_x b & + ∂_y a & &\:) \; dz \\ \end{alignedat} \right]\end{split}\]

Exterior Derivative of the Hodge Dual of a Rotation#

Applying in sequence the Hodge dual operator \(⋆\) and the exterior derivative operator \(d\) to the doubly covariant rotation \(d ⋆ R^{♭♭}\), we obtain:

Exterior Derivative of the Hodge Dual of Rotations in Differential Form

\[\begin{split}d( ⋆ R^{♭♭} ) = \left[ \begin{alignedat}{5} (& & + ∂_x a & + ∂_y b & + ∂_z c &\:) \; dx ∧ dy ∧ dz \\ (& + ∂_t a & & - ∂_y f & + ∂_z e &\:) \; dt ∧ dy ∧ dz \\ (& + ∂_t b & + ∂_x f & & - ∂_z d &\:) \; dt ∧ dz ∧ dx \\ (& + ∂_t c & - ∂_x e & + ∂_y d & &\:) \; dt ∧ dx ∧ dy \\ \end{alignedat} \right]\end{split}\]

Calculation steps

Take the exterior derivative

\[\begin{split}d(⋆R^{♭♭}) = d \begin{bmatrix} a \; dy ∧ dz \\ b \; dz ∧ dx \\ c \; dx ∧ dy \\ d \; dt ∧ dx \\ e \; dt ∧ dy \\ f \; dt ∧ dz \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Distribute the exterior derivative

\[\begin{split}d(⋆R^{♭♭}) = \begin{bmatrix} d(a \; dy ∧ dz) \\ d(b \; dz ∧ dx) \\ d(c \; dx ∧ dy) \\ d(d \; dt ∧ dx) \\ d(e \; dt ∧ dy) \\ d(f \; dt ∧ dz) \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Apply

\[\begin{split}d(⋆R^{♭♭}) = \begin{bmatrix} ∂_t (+a)\; dt ∧ dy ∧ dz) & ∂_x (+a)\; dx ∧ dy ∧ dz & & \\ ∂_t (+b)\; dt ∧ dz ∧ dx) & & ∂_y (+b)\; dy ∧ dz ∧ dx & \\ ∂_t (+c)\; dt ∧ dx ∧ dy) & & & ∂_z (+c)\; dz ∧ dx ∧ dy \\ & & ∂_y (+d)\; dy ∧ dt ∧ dx & ∂_z (+d)\; dz ∧ dt ∧ dx \\ & ∂_x (+e)\; dx ∧ dt ∧ dy & & ∂_z (+e)\; dz ∧ dt ∧ dy \\ & ∂_x (+f)\; dx ∧ dt ∧ dz & ∂_y (+f)\; dy ∧ dt ∧ dz & \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Reorder the 3-forms

\[\begin{split}d(⋆R^{♭♭}) = \begin{bmatrix} ∂_t (+a)(+1)\; dt ∧ dy ∧ dz & ∂_x (+a)(+1)\; dx ∧ dy ∧ dz & & \\ ∂_t (+b)(+1)\; dt ∧ dz ∧ dx & & ∂_y (+b)(+1)\; dx ∧ dy ∧ dz & \\ ∂_t (+c)(+1)\; dt ∧ dx ∧ dy & & & ∂_z (+c)(+1)\; dx ∧ dy ∧ dz \\ & & ∂_y (+d)(+1)\; dt ∧ dx ∧ dy & ∂_z (+d)(-1)\; dt ∧ dz ∧ dx \\ & ∂_x (+e)(-1)\; dt ∧ dx ∧ dy & & ∂_z (+e)(+1)\; dt ∧ dy ∧ dz \\ & ∂_x (+f)(+1)\; dt ∧ dz ∧ dx & ∂_y (+f)(-1)\; dt ∧ dy ∧ dz & \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Apply values

\[\begin{split}d(⋆R^{♭♭}) = \begin{bmatrix} ∂_t (+a)\; dt ∧ dy ∧ dz & ∂_x (+a)\; dx ∧ dy ∧ dz & & \\ ∂_t (+b)\; dt ∧ dz ∧ dx & & ∂_y (+b)\; dx ∧ dy ∧ dz & \\ ∂_t (+c)\; dt ∧ dx ∧ dy & & & ∂_z (+c)\; dx ∧ dy ∧ dz \\ & & ∂_y (+d)\; dt ∧ dx ∧ dy & ∂_z (-d)\; dt ∧ dz ∧ dx \\ & ∂_x (-e)\; dt ∧ dx ∧ dy & & ∂_z (+e)\; dt ∧ dy ∧ dz \\ & ∂_x (+f)\; dt ∧ dz ∧ dx & ∂_y (-f)\; dt ∧ dy ∧ dz & \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Organize the terms into a single column

Although the specific ordering is not mandatory. However, for clarity, the free matrix representation allows us to arrange the terms in a logical manner. Note that:

  • The first row excludes the terms with \(dt\)

  • The second row excludes the terms with \(dx\)

  • The third row excludes the terms with \(dy\)

  • The fourth row excludes the terms with \(dz\)

\[\begin{split}d( ⋆ R^{♭♭} ) = \left[ \begin{alignedat}{5} (& & + ∂_x a & + ∂_y b & + ∂_z c &\:) \; dx ∧ dy ∧ dz \\ (& + ∂_t a & & - ∂_y f & + ∂_z e &\:) \; dt ∧ dy ∧ dz \\ (& + ∂_t b & + ∂_x f & & - ∂_z d &\:) \; dt ∧ dz ∧ dx \\ (& + ∂_t c & - ∂_x e & + ∂_y d & &\:) \; dt ∧ dx ∧ dy \\ \end{alignedat} \right]\end{split}\]

Applying the Laplace-De Rham Operator#

In Minkowski space, the Laplace-De Rham operator is \(d ⋆ d ⋆ + ⋆ d ⋆ d\). Applied to a rotation in spacetime, we obtain the wave equations:

\[\begin{split}(d ⋆ d ⋆ + ⋆ d ⋆ d) \left[ \begin{aligned} - & a \; dt ∧ dx \\ - & b \; dt ∧ dy \\ - & c \; dt ∧ dz \\ & d \; dy ∧ dz \\ & e \; dz ∧ dx \\ & f \; dx ∧ dy \\ \end{aligned} \right] &= \left[ \begin{alignedat}{6} (&- ∂_t^2 a & + ∂_x^2 a & + ∂_y^2 a & + ∂_z^2 a &\:)&\: dt∧dx \\ (&- ∂_t^2 b & + ∂_x^2 b & + ∂_y^2 b & + ∂_z^2 b &\:)&\: dt∧dy \\ (&- ∂_t^2 c & + ∂_x^2 c & + ∂_y^2 c & + ∂_z^2 c &\:)&\: dt∧dz \\ (&+ ∂_t^2 d & - ∂_x^2 d & - ∂_y^2 d & - ∂_z^2 d &\:)&\: dy∧dz \\ (&+ ∂_t^2 f & - ∂_x^2 f & - ∂_y^2 f & - ∂_z^2 f &\:)&\: dx∧dy \\ (&+ ∂_t^2 e & - ∂_x^2 e & - ∂_y^2 e & - ∂_z^2 e &\:)&\: dz∧dx \\ \end{alignedat} \right]\end{split}\]
