The Exterior Derivative of Rotations in Spacetime#

by St√©phane Haussler

This article investigates the exterior derivative of rotations expressed in differential form. We employ the Cartan-Hodge formalism within the context of Minkowski spacetime. Following the systematic calculations presented in this article, I demonstrate in Of Maxwell Equations and Rotations that a twist in spacetime leads to the equations governing electromagnetism.

While the concept might not be entirely novel, I have not yet encountered some of my observations elsewhere. If you are aware of relevant references, feel free to open an issue and I will include them. I you identify any errors, you can either open an issue, or directly submit corrections via a merge request to my GitHub repository: Theoretical Universe.

Spacetime Rotations#

Rotations in spacetime can occur in six independent planes. Any rotation can be decomposed into a linear combination of basis rotations within each plane:

Rotation in Minkowski Space

\[\begin{split}R^{‚ôØ‚ôØ} = \begin{bmatrix} a \; ‚àÇ_t ‚àß ‚àÇ_x \\ b \; ‚àÇ_t ‚àß ‚àÇ_y \\ c \; ‚àÇ_t ‚àß ‚àÇ_z \\ d \; ‚àÇ_y ‚àß ‚àÇ_z \\ e \; ‚àÇ_z ‚àß ‚àÇ_x \\ f \; ‚àÇ_x ‚àß ‚àÇ_y \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Spacetime Rotations in Differential Forms#

The generic rotation above is doubly contravariant, given in terms of the wedge product \(‚àß\) of vectors corresponding to our physical understanding of space (and time). By fully flattening, we obtain the associated doubly covariant differential 2-form representative of the rotation:

Rotation in Differential Form

\[\begin{split}R^{‚ô≠‚ô≠} = \left[ \begin{aligned} - & a \; dt ‚àß dx \\ - & b \; dt ‚àß dy \\ - & c \; dt ‚àß dz \\ & d \; dy ‚àß dz \\ & e \; dz ‚àß dx \\ & f \; dx ‚àß dy \\ \end{aligned} \right]\end{split}\]

All Calculation Steps

Apply the flat operator \(flat\) to each components of the doubly contravariant rotation tensor \(R^{‚ôØ‚ôØ}\):

\[R^{‚ô≠‚ô≠} = (R^{‚ôØ‚ôØ})^{‚ô≠‚ô≠}\]

Expand the full expression and distribute the flat operators \(‚ô≠\):

\[\begin{split}R^{‚ô≠‚ô≠} = \begin{bmatrix} a \; ‚àÇ_t^‚ô≠ ‚àß ‚àÇ_x^‚ô≠ \\ b \; ‚àÇ_t^‚ô≠ ‚àß ‚àÇ_y^‚ô≠ \\ c \; ‚àÇ_t^‚ô≠ ‚àß ‚àÇ_z^‚ô≠ \\ d \; ‚àÇ_y^‚ô≠ ‚àß ‚àÇ_z^‚ô≠ \\ e \; ‚àÇ_z^‚ô≠ ‚àß ‚àÇ_x^‚ô≠ \\ f \; ‚àÇ_x^‚ô≠ ‚àß ‚àÇ_y^‚ô≠ \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Expand with the Minkowski metric:

\[\begin{split}R^{‚ô≠‚ô≠} = \begin{bmatrix} a \; Œ∑_{Œ±t} \; dx^Œ± ‚àß Œ∑_{Œ≤x} \; dx^Œ≤ \\ b \; Œ∑_{Œ±t} \; dx^Œ± ‚àß Œ∑_{Œ≤y} \; dx^Œ≤ \\ c \; Œ∑_{Œ±t} \; dx^Œ± ‚àß Œ∑_{Œ≤z} \; dx^Œ≤ \\ d \; Œ∑_{Œ±y} \; dx^Œ± ‚àß Œ∑_{Œ≤z} \; dx^Œ≤ \\ e \; Œ∑_{Œ±z} \; dx^Œ± ‚àß Œ∑_{Œ≤x} \; dx^Œ≤ \\ f \; Œ∑_{Œ±x} \; dx^Œ± ‚àß Œ∑_{Œ≤y} \; dx^Œ≤ \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

The wedge product \(‚àß\) is bilinear and the Minkowski metric components \(Œ∑\)‚Äôs can be taken in front of the expression:

\[\begin{split}R^{‚ô≠‚ô≠} = \begin{bmatrix} a \; Œ∑_{Œ±t} Œ∑_{Œ≤x} \; dx^Œ± ‚àß dx^Œ≤ \\ b \; Œ∑_{Œ±t} Œ∑_{Œ≤y} \; dx^Œ± ‚àß dx^Œ≤ \\ c \; Œ∑_{Œ±t} Œ∑_{Œ≤z} \; dx^Œ± ‚àß dx^Œ≤ \\ d \; Œ∑_{Œ±y} Œ∑_{Œ≤z} \; dx^Œ± ‚àß dx^Œ≤ \\ e \; Œ∑_{Œ±z} Œ∑_{Œ≤x} \; dx^Œ± ‚àß dx^Œ≤ \\ f \; Œ∑_{Œ±x} Œ∑_{Œ≤y} \; dx^Œ± ‚àß dx^Œ≤ \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Identify the non-zero components of the Minkowski metric \(Œ∑_{ŒºŒΩ}\):

\[\begin{split}R^{‚ô≠‚ô≠} = \begin{bmatrix} a \; Œ∑_{tt} Œ∑_{xx} \; dx^t ‚àß dx^x \\ b \; Œ∑_{tt} Œ∑_{yy} \; dx^t ‚àß dx^y \\ c \; Œ∑_{tt} Œ∑_{zz} \; dx^t ‚àß dx^z \\ d \; Œ∑_{yy} Œ∑_{zz} \; dx^y ‚àß dx^z \\ e \; Œ∑_{zz} Œ∑_{xx} \; dx^z ‚àß dx^x \\ f \; Œ∑_{xx} Œ∑_{yy} \; dx^x ‚àß dx^y \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

For readability, replace the \(dx^Œº\) symbols by their explicit expressions:

\[\begin{split}dx^t &= dt \\ dx^x &= dx \\ dx^y &= dy \\ dx^z &= dz\end{split}\]

We obtain:

\[\begin{split}R^{‚ô≠‚ô≠} = \begin{bmatrix} a \; Œ∑_{tt} Œ∑_{xx} \; dt ‚àß dx \\ b \; Œ∑_{tt} Œ∑_{yy} \; dt ‚àß dy \\ c \; Œ∑_{tt} Œ∑_{zz} \; dt ‚àß dz \\ d \; Œ∑_{yy} Œ∑_{zz} \; dy ‚àß dz \\ e \; Œ∑_{zz} Œ∑_{xx} \; dz ‚àß dx \\ f \; Œ∑_{xx} Œ∑_{yy} \; dx ‚àß dy \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Apply the numerical values of the Minkowski metric components \(Œ∑_{ŒºŒΩ}\):

\[\begin{split}R^{‚ô≠‚ô≠} = \begin{bmatrix} a \; (+1) (-1) \; dt ‚àß dx \\ b \; (+1) (-1) \; dt ‚àß dy \\ c \; (+1) (-1) \; dt ‚àß dz \\ d \; (-1) (-1) \; dy ‚àß dz \\ e \; (-1) (-1) \; dz ‚àß dx \\ f \; (-1) (-1) \; dx ‚àß dy \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Conclude:

\[\begin{split}R^{‚ô≠‚ô≠} = \left[ \begin{aligned} - & a \; dt ‚àß dx \\ - & b \; dt ‚àß dy \\ - & c \; dt ‚àß dz \\ & d \; dy ‚àß dz \\ & e \; dz ‚àß dx \\ & f \; dx ‚àß dy \\ \end{aligned} \right]\end{split}\]

The hodge dual is:

Hodge Dual of a Rotation in Differential Form

\[\begin{split}‚ãÜ R^{‚ô≠‚ô≠} = \begin{bmatrix} a \; dy ‚àß dz \\ b \; dz ‚àß dx \\ c \; dx ‚àß dy \\ d \; dt ‚àß dx \\ e \; dt ‚àß dy \\ f \; dt ‚àß dz \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Calculation steps

Take the Hodge dual

\[\begin{split}‚ãÜ R^{‚ô≠‚ô≠} = ‚ãÜ \left[ \begin{aligned} - a \; dt ‚àß dx \\ - b \; dt ‚àß dy \\ - c \; dt ‚àß dz \\ d \; dy ‚àß dz \\ e \; dz ‚àß dx \\ f \; dx ‚àß dy \\ \end{aligned} \right]\end{split}\]

Distribute the Hodge operator

\[\begin{split}‚ãÜ R^{‚ô≠‚ô≠} = \left[ \begin{aligned} - a \; ‚ãÜ (dt ‚àß dx) \\ - b \; ‚ãÜ (dt ‚àß dy) \\ - c \; ‚ãÜ (dt ‚àß dz) \\ d \; ‚ãÜ (dy ‚àß dz) \\ e \; ‚ãÜ (dz ‚àß dx) \\ f \; ‚ãÜ (dx ‚àß dy) \\ \end{aligned} \right]\end{split}\]

Apply Hodge dual to each basis elements

\[\begin{split}‚ãÜ R^{‚ô≠‚ô≠} = \left[ \begin{aligned} - a \; (-1) \; dy ‚àß dz \\ - b \; (-1) \; dz ‚àß dx \\ - c \; (-1) \; dx ‚àß dy \\ d \; (+1) \; dt ‚àß dx \\ e \; (+1) \; dt ‚àß dy \\ f \; (+1) \; dt ‚àß dz \\ \end{aligned} \right]\end{split}\]

Conclude

\[\begin{split}‚ãÜ R^{‚ô≠‚ô≠} = \begin{bmatrix} a \; dy ‚àß dz \\ b \; dz ‚àß dx \\ c \; dx ‚àß dy \\ d \; dt ‚àß dx \\ e \; dt ‚àß dy \\ f \; dt ‚àß dz \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Exterior Derivative of a Rotation#

Applying in sequence the exterior derivative operator \(d\) and the Hodge dual operator \(‚ãÜ\) to the doubly covariant rotation \(‚ãÜ d R^{‚ô≠‚ô≠}\), we obtain:

Hodge Dual of the Exterior Derivative of Rotations in Differential Form

\[\begin{split}‚ãÜ (dR^{‚ô≠‚ô≠}) = \left[ \begin{alignedat}{5} (& & - ‚àÇ_x d & - ‚àÇ_y e & - ‚àÇ_z f &\:) \; dt \\ (& - ‚àÇ_t d & & - ‚àÇ_y c & + ‚àÇ_z b &\:) \; dx \\ (& - ‚àÇ_t e & + ‚àÇ_x c & & - ‚àÇ_z a &\:) \; dy \\ (& - ‚àÇ_t f & - ‚àÇ_x b & + ‚àÇ_y a & &\:) \; dz \\ \end{alignedat} \right]\end{split}\]

Calculation steps

Distribute the exterior derivative

\[\begin{split}dR^{‚ô≠‚ô≠} = \begin{bmatrix} d( - a \; dt ‚àß dx ) \\ d( - b \; dt ‚àß dy ) \\ d( - c \; dt ‚àß dz ) \\ d( + d \; dy ‚àß dz ) \\ d( + e \; dz ‚àß dx ) \\ d( + f \; dx ‚àß dy ) \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Apply the exterior derivative

\[\begin{split}dR^{‚ô≠‚ô≠} = \begin{bmatrix} & & ‚àÇ_y (-a)\; dy ‚àß dt ‚àß dx & ‚àÇ_z (-a)\; dz ‚àß dt ‚àß dx \\ & ‚àÇ_x (-b)\; dx ‚àß dt ‚àß dy & & ‚àÇ_z (-b)\; dz ‚àß dt ‚àß dy \\ & ‚àÇ_x (-c)\; dx ‚àß dt ‚àß dz & ‚àÇ_y (-c)\; dy ‚àß dt ‚àß dz & \\ ‚àÇ_t (+d)\; dt ‚àß dy ‚àß dz & ‚àÇ_x (+d)\; dx ‚àß dy ‚àß dz & & \\ ‚àÇ_t (+e)\; dt ‚àß dz ‚àß dx & & ‚àÇ_y (+e)\; dy ‚àß dz ‚àß dx & \\ ‚àÇ_t (+f)\; dt ‚àß dx ‚àß dy & & & ‚àÇ_z (+f)\; dz ‚àß dx ‚àß dy \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Reorder exterior products, flipping the sign for every odd permutations

\[\begin{split}dR^{‚ô≠‚ô≠} = \begin{bmatrix} & & ‚àÇ_y (-a)(+1)\; dt‚àßdx‚àßdy & ‚àÇ_z (-a)(-1)\; dt‚àßdz‚àßdx \\ & ‚àÇ_x (-b)(-1)\; dt‚àßdx‚àßdy & & ‚àÇ_z (-b)(+1)\; dt‚àßdy‚àßdz \\ & ‚àÇ_x (-c)(+1)\; dt‚àßdz‚àßdx & ‚àÇ_y (-c)(-1)\; dt‚àßdy‚àßdz & \\ ‚àÇ_t (+d)(+1)\; dt‚àßdy‚àß dz & ‚àÇ_x (+d)(+1)\; dx‚àßdy‚àßdz & & \\ ‚àÇ_t (+e)(+1)\; dt‚àßdz‚àß dx & & ‚àÇ_y (+e)(+1)\; dx‚àßdy‚àßdz & \\ ‚àÇ_t (+f)(+1)\; dt‚àßdx‚àß dy & & & ‚àÇ_z (+f)(+1)\; dx‚àßdy‚àßdz \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Simplify

\[\begin{split}dR^{‚ô≠‚ô≠} = \begin{bmatrix} & & ‚àÇ_y (-a)\; dt ‚àß dx ‚àß dy & ‚àÇ_z (+a)\; dt ‚àß dz ‚àß dx \\ & ‚àÇ_x (+b)\; dt ‚àß dx ‚àß dy & & ‚àÇ_z (-b)\; dt ‚àß dy ‚àß dz \\ & ‚àÇ_x (-c)\; dt ‚àß dz ‚àß dx & ‚àÇ_y (+c)\; dt ‚àß dy ‚àß dz & \\ ‚àÇ_t (+d)\; dt ‚àß dy‚àß dz & ‚àÇ_x (+d)\; dx ‚àß dy ‚àß dz & & \\ ‚àÇ_t (+e)\; dt ‚àß dz‚àß dx & & ‚àÇ_y (+e)\; dx ‚àß dy ‚àß dz & \\ ‚àÇ_t (+f)\; dt ‚àß dx‚àß dy & & & ‚àÇ_z (+f)\; dx ‚àß dy ‚àß dz \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Organize the terms into a single column

Although the specific ordering is not mandatory. However, for clarity, the free matrix representation allows us to arrange the terms in a logical manner. Note that:

  • The first row excludes the terms with \(dt\)

  • The second row excludes the terms with \(dx\)

  • The third row excludes the terms with \(dy\)

  • The fourth row excludes the terms with \(dz\)

\[\begin{split}dR = \left[ \begin{alignedat}{5} (& & + ‚àÇ_x d & + ‚àÇ_y e & + ‚àÇ_z f &\:) \; dx^x ‚àß dx^y ‚àß dx^z \\ (& + ‚àÇ_t d & & + ‚àÇ_y c & - ‚àÇ_z b &\:) \; dx^t ‚àß dx^y ‚àß dx^z \\ (& + ‚àÇ_t e & - ‚àÇ_x c & & + ‚àÇ_z a &\:) \; dx^t ‚àß dx^z ‚àß dx^x \\ (& + ‚àÇ_t f & + ‚àÇ_x b & - ‚àÇ_y a & &\:) \; dx^t ‚àß dx^x ‚àß dx^y \\ \end{alignedat} \right]\end{split}\]

Apply the Hodge dual, thereby converting 3-forms to 1-forms:

\[\begin{split}‚ãÜ(dR) = \left[ \begin{alignedat}{5} (& & + ‚àÇ_x d & + ‚àÇ_y e & + ‚àÇ_z f &\:) \; (-dt) \\ (& + ‚àÇ_t d & & + ‚àÇ_y c & - ‚àÇ_z b &\:) \; (-dx) \\ (& + ‚àÇ_t e & - ‚àÇ_x c & & + ‚àÇ_z a &\:) \; (-dy) \\ (& + ‚àÇ_t f & + ‚àÇ_x b & - ‚àÇ_y a & &\:) \; (-dz) \\ \end{alignedat} \right]\end{split}\]

Conclude

\[\begin{split}‚ãÜ (dR) = \left[ \begin{alignedat}{5} (& & - ‚àÇ_x d & - ‚àÇ_y e & - ‚àÇ_z f &\:) \; dt \\ (& - ‚àÇ_t d & & - ‚àÇ_y c & + ‚àÇ_z b &\:) \; dx \\ (& - ‚àÇ_t e & + ‚àÇ_x c & & - ‚àÇ_z a &\:) \; dy \\ (& - ‚àÇ_t f & - ‚àÇ_x b & + ‚àÇ_y a & &\:) \; dz \\ \end{alignedat} \right]\end{split}\]

Exterior Derivative of the Hodge Dual of a Rotation#

Applying in sequence the Hodge dual operator \(‚ãÜ\) and the exterior derivative operator \(d\) to the doubly covariant rotation \(d ‚ãÜ R^{‚ô≠‚ô≠}\), we obtain:

Exterior Derivative of the Hodge Dual of Rotations in Differential Form

\[\begin{split}d( ‚ãÜ R^{‚ô≠‚ô≠} ) = \left[ \begin{alignedat}{5} (& & + ‚àÇ_x a & + ‚àÇ_y b & + ‚àÇ_z c &\:) \; dx ‚àß dy ‚àß dz \\ (& + ‚àÇ_t a & & - ‚àÇ_y f & + ‚àÇ_z e &\:) \; dt ‚àß dy ‚àß dz \\ (& + ‚àÇ_t b & + ‚àÇ_x f & & - ‚àÇ_z d &\:) \; dt ‚àß dz ‚àß dx \\ (& + ‚àÇ_t c & - ‚àÇ_x e & + ‚àÇ_y d & &\:) \; dt ‚àß dx ‚àß dy \\ \end{alignedat} \right]\end{split}\]

Calculation steps

Take the exterior derivative

\[\begin{split}d(‚ãÜR^{‚ô≠‚ô≠}) = d \begin{bmatrix} a \; dy ‚àß dz \\ b \; dz ‚àß dx \\ c \; dx ‚àß dy \\ d \; dt ‚àß dx \\ e \; dt ‚àß dy \\ f \; dt ‚àß dz \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Distribute the exterior derivative

\[\begin{split}d(‚ãÜR^{‚ô≠‚ô≠}) = \begin{bmatrix} d(a \; dy ‚àß dz) \\ d(b \; dz ‚àß dx) \\ d(c \; dx ‚àß dy) \\ d(d \; dt ‚àß dx) \\ d(e \; dt ‚àß dy) \\ d(f \; dt ‚àß dz) \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Apply

\[\begin{split}d(‚ãÜR^{‚ô≠‚ô≠}) = \begin{bmatrix} ‚àÇ_t (+a)\; dt ‚àß dy ‚àß dz) & ‚àÇ_x (+a)\; dx ‚àß dy ‚àß dz & & \\ ‚àÇ_t (+b)\; dt ‚àß dz ‚àß dx) & & ‚àÇ_y (+b)\; dy ‚àß dz ‚àß dx & \\ ‚àÇ_t (+c)\; dt ‚àß dx ‚àß dy) & & & ‚àÇ_z (+c)\; dz ‚àß dx ‚àß dy \\ & & ‚àÇ_y (+d)\; dy ‚àß dt ‚àß dx & ‚àÇ_z (+d)\; dz ‚àß dt ‚àß dx \\ & ‚àÇ_x (+e)\; dx ‚àß dt ‚àß dy & & ‚àÇ_z (+e)\; dz ‚àß dt ‚àß dy \\ & ‚àÇ_x (+f)\; dx ‚àß dt ‚àß dz & ‚àÇ_y (+f)\; dy ‚àß dt ‚àß dz & \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Reorder the 3-forms

\[\begin{split}d(‚ãÜR^{‚ô≠‚ô≠}) = \begin{bmatrix} ‚àÇ_t (+a)(+1)\; dt ‚àß dy ‚àß dz & ‚àÇ_x (+a)(+1)\; dx ‚àß dy ‚àß dz & & \\ ‚àÇ_t (+b)(+1)\; dt ‚àß dz ‚àß dx & & ‚àÇ_y (+b)(+1)\; dx ‚àß dy ‚àß dz & \\ ‚àÇ_t (+c)(+1)\; dt ‚àß dx ‚àß dy & & & ‚àÇ_z (+c)(+1)\; dx ‚àß dy ‚àß dz \\ & & ‚àÇ_y (+d)(+1)\; dt ‚àß dx ‚àß dy & ‚àÇ_z (+d)(-1)\; dt ‚àß dz ‚àß dx \\ & ‚àÇ_x (+e)(-1)\; dt ‚àß dx ‚àß dy & & ‚àÇ_z (+e)(+1)\; dt ‚àß dy ‚àß dz \\ & ‚àÇ_x (+f)(+1)\; dt ‚àß dz ‚àß dx & ‚àÇ_y (+f)(-1)\; dt ‚àß dy ‚àß dz & \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Apply values

\[\begin{split}d(‚ãÜR^{‚ô≠‚ô≠}) = \begin{bmatrix} ‚àÇ_t (+a)\; dt ‚àß dy ‚àß dz & ‚àÇ_x (+a)\; dx ‚àß dy ‚àß dz & & \\ ‚àÇ_t (+b)\; dt ‚àß dz ‚àß dx & & ‚àÇ_y (+b)\; dx ‚àß dy ‚àß dz & \\ ‚àÇ_t (+c)\; dt ‚àß dx ‚àß dy & & & ‚àÇ_z (+c)\; dx ‚àß dy ‚àß dz \\ & & ‚àÇ_y (+d)\; dt ‚àß dx ‚àß dy & ‚àÇ_z (-d)\; dt ‚àß dz ‚àß dx \\ & ‚àÇ_x (-e)\; dt ‚àß dx ‚àß dy & & ‚àÇ_z (+e)\; dt ‚àß dy ‚àß dz \\ & ‚àÇ_x (+f)\; dt ‚àß dz ‚àß dx & ‚àÇ_y (-f)\; dt ‚àß dy ‚àß dz & \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Organize the terms into a single column

Although the specific ordering is not mandatory. However, for clarity, the free matrix representation allows us to arrange the terms in a logical manner. Note that:

  • The first row excludes the terms with \(dt\)

  • The second row excludes the terms with \(dx\)

  • The third row excludes the terms with \(dy\)

  • The fourth row excludes the terms with \(dz\)

\[\begin{split}d( ‚ãÜ R^{‚ô≠‚ô≠} ) = \left[ \begin{alignedat}{5} (& & + ‚àÇ_x a & + ‚àÇ_y b & + ‚àÇ_z c &\:) \; dx ‚àß dy ‚àß dz \\ (& + ‚àÇ_t a & & - ‚àÇ_y f & + ‚àÇ_z e &\:) \; dt ‚àß dy ‚àß dz \\ (& + ‚àÇ_t b & + ‚àÇ_x f & & - ‚àÇ_z d &\:) \; dt ‚àß dz ‚àß dx \\ (& + ‚àÇ_t c & - ‚àÇ_x e & + ‚àÇ_y d & &\:) \; dt ‚àß dx ‚àß dy \\ \end{alignedat} \right]\end{split}\]

Applying the Laplace-De Rham Operator#

In Minkowski space, the Laplace-De Rham operator is \(d ‚ãÜ d ‚ãÜ + ‚ãÜ d ‚ãÜ d\). Applied to a rotation in spacetime, we obtain the wave equations:

\[\begin{split}(d ‚ãÜ d ‚ãÜ + ‚ãÜ d ‚ãÜ d) \left[ \begin{aligned} - & a \; dt ‚àß dx \\ - & b \; dt ‚àß dy \\ - & c \; dt ‚àß dz \\ & d \; dy ‚àß dz \\ & e \; dz ‚àß dx \\ & f \; dx ‚àß dy \\ \end{aligned} \right] &= \left[ \begin{alignedat}{6} (&- ‚àÇ_t^2 a & + ‚àÇ_x^2 a & + ‚àÇ_y^2 a & + ‚àÇ_z^2 a &\:)&\: dt‚àßdx \\ (&- ‚àÇ_t^2 b & + ‚àÇ_x^2 b & + ‚àÇ_y^2 b & + ‚àÇ_z^2 b &\:)&\: dt‚àßdy \\ (&- ‚àÇ_t^2 c & + ‚àÇ_x^2 c & + ‚àÇ_y^2 c & + ‚àÇ_z^2 c &\:)&\: dt‚àßdz \\ (&+ ‚àÇ_t^2 d & - ‚àÇ_x^2 d & - ‚àÇ_y^2 d & - ‚àÇ_z^2 d &\:)&\: dy‚àßdz \\ (&+ ‚àÇ_t^2 f & - ‚àÇ_x^2 f & - ‚àÇ_y^2 f & - ‚àÇ_z^2 f &\:)&\: dx‚àßdy \\ (&+ ‚àÇ_t^2 e & - ‚àÇ_x^2 e & - ‚àÇ_y^2 e & - ‚àÇ_z^2 e &\:)&\: dz‚àßdx \\ \end{alignedat} \right]\end{split}\]
