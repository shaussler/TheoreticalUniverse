Rotations in differential forms

Rotations in differential forms#

by Stéphane Haussler

Warning

Under construction

Since I have not seen anybody mentioning it or highlighting it, you can see the symmetry pop out of the Hodge start operator when ordering into row and columns of differential forms:

\[\begin{split}\begin{align} \star F^\flat &= \{ & F^z dx \wedge dy & \\ & & F^x dy \wedge dz \\ F^y dz \wedge dx & & \} \\ &= \frac{1}{2} \{ & +F^z dx \wedge dy & -F^y dx \wedge dz \\ -F^z dy \wedge dx & & +F^x dy \wedge dz \\ +F^y dz \wedge dx & -F^x dz \wedge dy & \} \end{align}\end{split}\]

With implicit vector and surface basis vectors, we obtain:

\[\begin{split}\star F^\flat = \star \{ F^x \\ F^y \\ F^z \}^\flat = \frac{1}{2} \{ & +F^z & -F^y \\ -F^z & & +F^x \\ +F^y & -F^x & \}^\flat\end{split}\]
