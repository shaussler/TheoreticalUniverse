All Electromagnetic Field Tensors#

by St√©phane Haussler

In this section, I systematically derive all representations of the Faraday tensor \(F\) and its Hodge dual with metric signature \((+,-,-,-)\). I cannot imagine there is no sign error in the calculations below. The Hodge dual \(‚ãÜ\:F\) is also noted \(G\). We take as a starting point the electromagnetic field 2-form derivated in the article Maxwell‚Äôs Equations via Differential Forms:

\[\begin{split}F^{‚ô≠‚ô≠} = \left[ \begin{aligned} - & \E^x \; dt ‚àß dx \\ - & \E^y \; dt ‚àß dy \\ - & \E^z \; dt ‚àß dz \\ & B^x \; dy ‚àß dz \\ & B^y \; dz ‚àß dx \\ & B^z \; dx ‚àß dy \\ \end{aligned} \right]\end{split}\]

All the expressions of the Faraday Tensor \(F\) are the same object viewed from a different perspective. All \(F\) objects are strictly equivalent, whether it is called a 2-form or a tensor, and a matter of convention.

\(F^{‚ô≠‚ô≠}\)#

The Faraday 2-form is here the starting point as given above. In a columns representation, it is written as:

\[\begin{split}F^{‚ô≠‚ô≠} = \left[ \begin{aligned} - & \E^x \; dt ‚àß dx \\ - & \E^y \; dt ‚àß dy \\ - & \E^z \; dt ‚àß dz \\ & B^x \; dy ‚àß dz \\ & B^y \; dz ‚àß dx \\ & B^z \; dx ‚àß dy \\ \end{aligned} \right] \\\end{split}\]

From the Faraday 2-form and noting that 2-forms are tensors, we can derive the row/column representation of the doubly covariant Faraday tensor.

\[\begin{split}F^{‚ô≠‚ô≠} = \frac{1}{2} \begin{bmatrix} & + \E^x \; dx ‚àß dt & + \E^y \; dy ‚àß dt & + \E^z \; dz ‚àß dt \\ - \E^x \; dt ‚àß dx & & - B^z \; dy ‚àß dx & + B^y \; dz ‚àß dx \\ - \E^y \; dt ‚àß dy & + B^z \; dx ‚àß dy & & - B^x \; dz ‚àß dy \\ - \E^z \; dt ‚àß dz & - B^y \; dx ‚àß dz & + B^x \; dy ‚àß dz & \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Calculations

Start from the Faraday 2-form

\[\begin{split}F^{‚ô≠‚ô≠} = \left[ \begin{aligned} - & \E^x \; dt ‚àß dx \\ - & \E^y \; dt ‚àß dy \\ - & \E^z \; dt ‚àß dz \\ & B^x \; dy ‚àß dz \\ & B^y \; dz ‚àß dx \\ & B^z \; dx ‚àß dy \\ \end{aligned} \right] \\\end{split}\]

Duplicate each 2-form

The following line is so obvious that the prospective reader may be confused. I am; indeed; really writing that \(A = \frac{1}{2} (A+A)\).

\[\begin{split}F^{‚ô≠‚ô≠} = \frac{1}{2} \begin{bmatrix} - \E^x \; dt ‚àß dx & - \E^x \; dt ‚àß dx \\ - \E^y \; dt ‚àß dy & - \E^y \; dt ‚àß dy \\ - \E^z \; dt ‚àß dz & - \E^z \; dt ‚àß dz \\ + B^x \; dy ‚àß dz & + B^x \; dy ‚àß dz \\ + B^y \; dz ‚àß dx & + B^y \; dz ‚àß dx \\ + B^z \; dx ‚àß dy & + B^z \; dx ‚àß dy \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Flip the exterior product

The purpose of the above operation was to utilize the antisymmetry of the exterior product and flip the signs \(dx^Œº ‚àß dx^ŒΩ = -dx^ŒΩ ‚àß dx^Œº\) as needed.

\[\begin{split}F^{‚ô≠‚ô≠} = \frac{1}{2} \begin{bmatrix} - \E^x \; dt ‚àß dx & + \E^x \; dx ‚àß dt \\ - \E^y \; dt ‚àß dy & + \E^y \; dy ‚àß dt \\ - \E^z \; dt ‚àß dz & + \E^z \; dz ‚àß dt \\ + B^x \; dy ‚àß dz & - B^x \; dz ‚àß dy \\ + B^y \; dz ‚àß dx & - B^y \; dx ‚àß dz \\ + B^z \; dx ‚àß dy & - B^z \; dy ‚àß dx \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

The purpose of this operation is to switch the representation of the Faraday 2-Form as a single row of basis 2-Forms, to a row/column representation.

Reorder into rows/column representation

From there, we conclude utilizing the free matrix representation of the Cartan-Hodge formalism, reordering the elements into rows and columns.

\[\begin{split}F^{‚ô≠‚ô≠} = \frac{1}{2} \begin{bmatrix} & + \E^x \; dx ‚àß dt & + \E^y \; dy ‚àß dt & + \E^z \; dz ‚àß dt \\ - \E^x \; dt ‚àß dx & & - B^z \; dy ‚àß dx & + B^y \; dz ‚àß dx \\ - \E^y \; dt ‚àß dy & + B^z \; dx ‚àß dy & & - B^x \; dz ‚àß dy \\ - \E^z \; dt ‚àß dz & - B^y \; dx ‚àß dz & + B^x \; dy ‚àß dz & \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

With implicit bivector basis, we have the standard representation with abstract index notation

\[\begin{split}F_{ŒºŒΩ} = \begin{bmatrix} & + \E^x & + \E^y & + \E^z \\ - \E^x & & - B^z & + B^y \\ - \E^y & + B^z & & - B^x \\ - \E^z & - B^y & + B^x & \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Where the field 2-form is related to the Faraday tensor with:

\[F^{‚ô≠‚ô≠} = \frac{1}{2} \: F_{ŒºŒΩ} \: dx^Œº ‚àß dx^ŒΩ\]

For sanity, I refer to Wikipedia for a quick double check of the link between the Faraday 2-Form and the Faraday tensor.

\(F^{‚ô≠‚ôØ}\)#

The starting point is the twice flattened Faraday tensor \(F^{‚ô≠‚ô≠}\) to which we apply the ‚ô≠‚ôØ operator \(F^{‚ô≠‚ôØ}=\left(F^{‚ô≠‚ô≠}\right)^{‚ô≠‚ôØ}\) and obtain:

\[\begin{split}F^{‚ô≠‚ôØ} = \left[ \begin{aligned} & \E^x \; dt ‚àß ‚àÇ_x \\ & \E^y \; dt ‚àß ‚àÇ_y \\ & \E^z \; dt ‚àß ‚àÇ_z \\ - & B^x \; dy ‚àß ‚àÇ_z \\ - & B^y \; dz ‚àß ‚àÇ_x \\ - & B^z \; dx ‚àß ‚àÇ_y \\ \end{aligned} \right]\end{split}\]

Calculations

Start from the Faraday 2-form

\[\begin{split}F^{‚ô≠‚ô≠} = \left[ \begin{aligned} - & \E^x \; dt ‚àß dx \\ - & \E^y \; dt ‚àß dy \\ - & \E^z \; dt ‚àß dz \\ & B^x \; dy ‚àß dz \\ & B^y \; dz ‚àß dx \\ & B^z \; dx ‚àß dy \\ \end{aligned} \right] \\\end{split}\]

Distribute the flat ‚ô≠ and sharp ‚ôØ operators

\[\begin{split}F^{‚ô≠‚ôØ} = \left(F^{‚ô≠‚ô≠}\right)^{‚ô≠‚ôØ} = \left[ \begin{aligned} - & \E^x \; dt ‚àß dx \\ - & \E^y \; dt ‚àß dy \\ - & \E^z \; dt ‚àß dz \\ & B^x \; dy ‚àß dz \\ & B^y \; dz ‚àß dx \\ & B^z \; dx ‚àß dy \\ \end{aligned} \right]^{‚ô≠‚ôØ} = \left[ \begin{aligned} - & \E^x \; dt^‚ô≠ ‚àß dx^‚ôØ \\ - & \E^y \; dt^‚ô≠ ‚àß dy^‚ôØ \\ - & \E^z \; dt^‚ô≠ ‚àß dz^‚ôØ \\ & B^x \; dy^‚ô≠ ‚àß dz^‚ôØ \\ & B^y \; dz^‚ô≠ ‚àß dx^‚ôØ \\ & B^z \; dx^‚ô≠ ‚àß dy^‚ôØ \\ \end{aligned} \right]\end{split}\]

Expand the sharpened basis covectors

The \(dx^Œº\) terms are already flattened, and applying the flattening operator twice does not modify these terms: \((dx^Œº)^‚ô≠=dx^Œº\). The sharpened terms are expanded with the metric: \((dx^ŒΩ)^‚ôØ = Œ∑_{ŒΩŒº} ‚àÇ_Œº\).

\[\begin{split}F^{‚ô≠‚ôØ} = \left[ \begin{aligned} - & \E^x \; dt ‚àß Œ∑^{xŒº} ‚àÇ_Œº \\ - & \E^y \; dt ‚àß Œ∑^{yŒº} ‚àÇ_Œº \\ - & \E^z \; dt ‚àß Œ∑^{zŒº} ‚àÇ_Œº \\ & B^x \; dy ‚àß Œ∑^{zŒº} ‚àÇ_Œº \\ & B^y \; dz ‚àß Œ∑^{xŒº} ‚àÇ_Œº \\ & B^z \; dx ‚àß Œ∑^{yŒº} ‚àÇ_Œº \\ \end{aligned} \right]\end{split}\]

Identify the non-zero terms

\[\begin{split}F^{‚ô≠‚ôØ} = \left[ \begin{aligned} - & \E^x \; dt ‚àß Œ∑^{xx} ‚àÇ_x \\ - & \E^y \; dt ‚àß Œ∑^{yy} ‚àÇ_y \\ - & \E^z \; dt ‚àß Œ∑^{zz} ‚àÇ_z \\ & B^x \; dy ‚àß Œ∑^{zz} ‚àÇ_z \\ & B^y \; dz ‚àß Œ∑^{xx} ‚àÇ_x \\ & B^z \; dx ‚àß Œ∑^{yy} ‚àÇ_y \\ \end{aligned} \right]\end{split}\]

Apply numerical values

\[\begin{split}F^{‚ô≠‚ôØ} = \left[ \begin{aligned} - & \E^x \; dt ‚àß (-1) ‚àÇ_x \\ - & \E^y \; dt ‚àß (-1) ‚àÇ_y \\ - & \E^z \; dt ‚àß (-1) ‚àÇ_z \\ & B^x \; dy ‚àß (-1) ‚àÇ_z \\ & B^y \; dz ‚àß (-1) ‚àÇ_x \\ & B^z \; dx ‚àß (-1) ‚àÇ_y \\ \end{aligned} \right] = \left[ \begin{aligned} & \E^x \; dt ‚àß ‚àÇ_x \\ & \E^y \; dt ‚àß ‚àÇ_y \\ & \E^z \; dt ‚àß ‚àÇ_z \\ - & B^x \; dy ‚àß ‚àÇ_z \\ - & B^y \; dz ‚àß ‚àÇ_x \\ - & B^z \; dx ‚àß ‚àÇ_y \\ \end{aligned} \right]\end{split}\]

We derive the row/column representation of the \(F^{‚ô≠‚ôØ}\) Faraday tensor:

\[\begin{split}F^{‚ô≠‚ôØ} = \frac{1}{2} \begin{bmatrix} & + \E^x \; dt ‚àß ‚àÇ_x & + \E^y \; dt ‚àß ‚àÇ_y & + \E^z \; dt ‚àß ‚àÇ_z \\ + \E^x \; dx ‚àß ‚àÇ_t & & - B^z \; dx ‚àß ‚àÇ_y & + B^y \; dx ‚àß ‚àÇ_z \\ + \E^y \; dy ‚àß ‚àÇ_t & + B^z \; dy ‚àß ‚àÇ_x & & - B^x \; dy ‚àß ‚àÇ_z \\ + \E^z \; dz ‚àß ‚àÇ_t & - B^y \; dz ‚àß ‚àÇ_x & + B^x \; dz ‚àß ‚àÇ_y & \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Reorder in row/column convention

\[\begin{split}F^{‚ô≠‚ôØ} = \frac{1}{2} \left[ \begin{aligned} & + \E^x \; dt ‚àß ‚àÇ_x & + \E^y \; dt ‚àß ‚àÇ_y & + \E^z \; dt ‚àß ‚àÇ_z \\ + \E^x \; dx ‚àß ‚àÇ_t & & - B^z \; dx ‚àß ‚àÇ_y & + B^y \; dx ‚àß ‚àÇ_z \\ + \E^y \; dy ‚àß ‚àÇ_t & + B^z \; dy ‚àß ‚àÇ_x & & - B^x \; dy ‚àß ‚àÇ_z \\ + \E^z \; dz ‚àß ‚àÇ_t & - B^y \; dz ‚àß ‚àÇ_x & + B^x \; dz ‚àß ‚àÇ_y & \\ \end{aligned} \right]\end{split}\]

With implicit bivector basis, we have :

\[\begin{split}F_Œº{}^ŒΩ = \begin{bmatrix} & + \E^x & + \E^y & + \E^z \\ + \E^x & & - B^z & + B^y \\ + \E^y & + B^z & & - B^x \\ + \E^z & - B^y & + B^x & \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

\[F^{‚ô≠‚ôØ} = \frac{1}{2} \: F_Œº{}^ŒΩ \: dx^Œº ‚àß ‚àÇ_ŒΩ\]

\(F^{‚ôØ‚ôØ}\)#

The starting point is the twice flattened Faraday tensor \(F^{‚ô≠‚ô≠}\) to which we apply the ‚ô≠‚ôØ operator \(F^{‚ô≠‚ôØ}=\left(F^{‚ô≠‚ô≠}\right)^{‚ô≠‚ôØ}\) and obtain:

\[\begin{split}F^{‚ôØ‚ôØ} = \left[ \begin{aligned} \E^x \; ‚àÇ_t ‚àß ‚àÇ_x \\ \E^y \; ‚àÇ_t ‚àß ‚àÇ_y \\ \E^z \; ‚àÇ_t ‚àß ‚àÇ_z \\ B^x \; ‚àÇ_y ‚àß ‚àÇ_z \\ B^y \; ‚àÇ_z ‚àß ‚àÇ_x \\ B^z \; ‚àÇ_x ‚àß ‚àÇ_y \\ \end{aligned} \right]\end{split}\]

Calculations

Start from the Faraday 2-form

\[\begin{split}F^{‚ô≠‚ô≠} = \left[ \begin{aligned} - & \E^x \; dt ‚àß dx \\ - & \E^y \; dt ‚àß dy \\ - & \E^z \; dt ‚àß dz \\ & B^x \; dy ‚àß dz \\ & B^y \; dz ‚àß dx \\ & B^z \; dx ‚àß dy \\ \end{aligned} \right]\end{split}\]

Apply the musical sharp-sharp ‚ôØ‚ôØ operator

\[\begin{split}F^{‚ôØ‚ôØ} = \left(F^{‚ô≠‚ô≠} \right)^{‚ôØ‚ôØ} = \left[ \begin{aligned} - & \E^x \; dt ‚àß dx \\ - & \E^y \; dt ‚àß dy \\ - & \E^z \; dt ‚àß dz \\ & B^x \; dy ‚àß dz \\ & B^y \; dz ‚àß dx \\ & B^z \; dx ‚àß dy \\ \end{aligned} \right]^{‚ôØ‚ôØ}\end{split}\]

Distribute the musical operators

\[\begin{split}F^{‚ôØ‚ôØ} = \left[ \begin{aligned} - & \E^x \; (dt ‚àß dx)^{‚ôØ‚ôØ} \\ - & \E^y \; (dt ‚àß dy)^{‚ôØ‚ôØ} \\ - & \E^z \; (dt ‚àß dz)^{‚ôØ‚ôØ} \\ & B^x \; (dy ‚àß dz)^{‚ôØ‚ôØ} \\ & B^y \; (dz ‚àß dx)^{‚ôØ‚ôØ} \\ & B^z \; (dx ‚àß dy)^{‚ôØ‚ôØ} \\ \end{aligned} \right]\end{split}\]

Distribute the musical operators

\[\begin{split}F^{‚ôØ‚ôØ} = \left[ \begin{aligned} - & \E^x \; dt^‚ôØ ‚àß dx^‚ôØ \\ - & \E^y \; dt^‚ôØ ‚àß dy^‚ôØ \\ - & \E^z \; dt^‚ôØ ‚àß dz^‚ôØ \\ & B^x \; dy^‚ôØ ‚àß dz^‚ôØ \\ & B^y \; dz^‚ôØ ‚àß dx^‚ôØ \\ & B^z \; dx^‚ôØ ‚àß dy^‚ôØ \\ \end{aligned} \right]\end{split}\]

Apply

\[\begin{split}F^{‚ôØ‚ôØ} = \left[ \begin{aligned} - & \E^x \; Œ∑^{tŒº} ‚àÇ_Œº ‚àß Œ∑^{xŒº} ‚àÇ_Œº \\ - & \E^y \; Œ∑^{tŒº} ‚àÇ_Œº ‚àß Œ∑^{yŒº} ‚àÇ_Œº \\ - & \E^z \; Œ∑^{tŒº} ‚àÇ_Œº ‚àß Œ∑^{zŒº} ‚àÇ_Œº \\ & B^x \; Œ∑^{yŒº} ‚àÇ_Œº ‚àß Œ∑^{zŒº} ‚àÇ_Œº \\ & B^y \; Œ∑^{zŒº} ‚àÇ_Œº ‚àß Œ∑^{xŒº} ‚àÇ_Œº \\ & B^z \; Œ∑^{xŒº} ‚àÇ_Œº ‚àß Œ∑^{yŒº} ‚àÇ_Œº \\ \end{aligned} \right]\end{split}\]

Identify non-zero terms

\[\begin{split}F^{‚ôØ‚ôØ} = \left[ \begin{aligned} - & \E^x \; Œ∑^{tt} ‚àÇ_t ‚àß Œ∑^{xx} ‚àÇ_x \\ - & \E^y \; Œ∑^{tt} ‚àÇ_t ‚àß Œ∑^{yy} ‚àÇ_y \\ - & \E^z \; Œ∑^{tt} ‚àÇ_t ‚àß Œ∑^{zz} ‚àÇ_z \\ & B^x \; Œ∑^{yy} ‚àÇ_y ‚àß Œ∑^{zz} ‚àÇ_z \\ & B^y \; Œ∑^{zz} ‚àÇ_z ‚àß Œ∑^{xx} ‚àÇ_x \\ & B^z \; Œ∑^{xx} ‚àÇ_x ‚àß Œ∑^{yy} ‚àÇ_y \\ \end{aligned} \right]\end{split}\]

Apply numerical values

\[\begin{split}F^{‚ôØ‚ôØ} = \left[ \begin{aligned} - & \E^x \; (+1) ‚àÇ_t ‚àß (-1) ‚àÇ_x \\ - & \E^y \; (+1) ‚àÇ_t ‚àß (-1) ‚àÇ_y \\ - & \E^z \; (+1) ‚àÇ_t ‚àß (-1) ‚àÇ_z \\ & B^x \; (-1) ‚àÇ_y ‚àß (-1) ‚àÇ_z \\ & B^y \; (-1) ‚àÇ_z ‚àß (-1) ‚àÇ_x \\ & B^z \; (-1) ‚àÇ_x ‚àß (-1) ‚àÇ_y \\ \end{aligned} \right]\end{split}\]

Conclude

\[\begin{split}F^{‚ôØ‚ôØ} = \left[ \begin{aligned} \E^x \; ‚àÇ_t ‚àß ‚àÇ_x \\ \E^y \; ‚àÇ_t ‚àß ‚àÇ_y \\ \E^z \; ‚àÇ_t ‚àß ‚àÇ_z \\ B^x \; ‚àÇ_y ‚àß ‚àÇ_z \\ B^y \; ‚àÇ_z ‚àß ‚àÇ_x \\ B^z \; ‚àÇ_x ‚àß ‚àÇ_y \\ \end{aligned} \right]\end{split}\]

We derive the row/column representation of the \(F^{‚ô≠‚ôØ}\) Faraday tensor:

\[\begin{split}F^{‚ôØ‚ôØ} = \frac{1}{2} \begin{bmatrix} & - \E^x \; ‚àÇ_x ‚àß ‚àÇ_t & - \E^y \; ‚àÇ_y ‚àß ‚àÇ_t & - \E^z \; ‚àÇ_z ‚àß ‚àÇ_t \\ + \E^x \; ‚àÇ_t ‚àß ‚àÇ_x & & - B^z \; ‚àÇ_y ‚àß ‚àÇ_x & + B^y \; ‚àÇ_z ‚àß ‚àÇ_x \\ + \E^y \; ‚àÇ_t ‚àß ‚àÇ_y & + B^z \; ‚àÇ_x ‚àß ‚àÇ_y & & - B^x \; ‚àÇ_z ‚àß ‚àÇ_y \\ + \E^z \; ‚àÇ_t ‚àß ‚àÇ_z & - B^y \; ‚àÇ_x ‚àß ‚àÇ_z & + B^x \; ‚àÇ_y ‚àß ‚àÇ_z & \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Calculations

Start from

\[\begin{split}F^{‚ôØ‚ôØ} = \left[ \begin{aligned} \E^x \; ‚àÇ_t ‚àß ‚àÇ_x \\ \E^y \; ‚àÇ_t ‚àß ‚àÇ_y \\ \E^z \; ‚àÇ_t ‚àß ‚àÇ_z \\ B^x \; ‚àÇ_y ‚àß ‚àÇ_z \\ B^y \; ‚àÇ_z ‚àß ‚àÇ_x \\ B^z \; ‚àÇ_x ‚àß ‚àÇ_y \\ \end{aligned} \right]\end{split}\]

Apply the symmetries of the exterior product

\[\begin{split}F^{‚ôØ‚ôØ} = \left[ \begin{aligned} \E^x \; \frac{1}{2} & (‚àÇ_t ‚àß ‚àÇ_x - ‚àÇ_x ‚àß ‚àÇ_t) \\ \E^y \; \frac{1}{2} & (‚àÇ_t ‚àß ‚àÇ_y - ‚àÇ_y ‚àß ‚àÇ_t) \\ \E^z \; \frac{1}{2} & (‚àÇ_t ‚àß ‚àÇ_z - ‚àÇ_z ‚àß ‚àÇ_t) \\ B^x \; \frac{1}{2} & (‚àÇ_y ‚àß ‚àÇ_z - ‚àÇ_z ‚àß ‚àÇ_y) \\ B^y \; \frac{1}{2} & (‚àÇ_z ‚àß ‚àÇ_x - ‚àÇ_x ‚àß ‚àÇ_z) \\ B^z \; \frac{1}{2} & (‚àÇ_x ‚àß ‚àÇ_y - ‚àÇ_y ‚àß ‚àÇ_x) \\ \end{aligned} \right]\end{split}\]

Reorder

\[\begin{split}F^{‚ôØ‚ôØ} = \frac{1}{2} \left[ \begin{aligned} \E^x \; ‚àÇ_t ‚àß ‚àÇ_x & - \E^x \; ‚àÇ_x ‚àß ‚àÇ_t \\ \E^y \; ‚àÇ_t ‚àß ‚àÇ_y & - \E^y \; ‚àÇ_y ‚àß ‚àÇ_t \\ \E^z \; ‚àÇ_t ‚àß ‚àÇ_z & - \E^z \; ‚àÇ_z ‚àß ‚àÇ_t \\ B^x \; ‚àÇ_y ‚àß ‚àÇ_z & - B^x \; ‚àÇ_z ‚àß ‚àÇ_y \\ B^y \; ‚àÇ_z ‚àß ‚àÇ_x & - B^y \; ‚àÇ_x ‚àß ‚àÇ_z \\ B^z \; ‚àÇ_x ‚àß ‚àÇ_y & - B^z \; ‚àÇ_y ‚àß ‚àÇ_x \\ \end{aligned} \right]\end{split}\]

Reorder and conclude

\[\begin{split}F^{‚ôØ‚ôØ} = \frac{1}{2} \begin{bmatrix} & - \E^x \; ‚àÇ_x ‚àß ‚àÇ_t & - \E^y \; ‚àÇ_y ‚àß ‚àÇ_t & - \E^z \; ‚àÇ_z ‚àß ‚àÇ_t \\ \E^x \; ‚àÇ_t ‚àß ‚àÇ_x & & - B^z \; ‚àÇ_y ‚àß ‚àÇ_x & + B^y \; ‚àÇ_z ‚àß ‚àÇ_x \\ \E^y \; ‚àÇ_t ‚àß ‚àÇ_y & + B^z \; ‚àÇ_x ‚àß ‚àÇ_y & & - B^x \; ‚àÇ_z ‚àß ‚àÇ_y \\ \E^z \; ‚àÇ_t ‚àß ‚àÇ_z & - B^y \; ‚àÇ_x ‚àß ‚àÇ_z & + B^x \; ‚àÇ_y ‚àß ‚àÇ_z & \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

With implicit bivector basis, we have the standard representation with abstract index notation, which also permits to verify the calculations here:

\[\begin{split}F_{ŒºŒΩ} = \begin{bmatrix} & - \E^x & - \E^y & - \E^z \\ + \E^x & & - B^z & + B^y \\ + \E^y & + B^z & & - B^x \\ + \E^z & - B^y & + B^x & \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Where the electromagnetic field is related to the doubly contravariant Faraday tensor through:

\[F^{‚ôØ‚ôØ} = \frac{1}{2} \: F^{ŒºŒΩ} \: ‚àÇ_Œº ‚àß ‚àÇ_ŒΩ\]

\(F^{‚ôØ‚ô≠}\)#

\(G^{‚ô≠‚ô≠}\)#

The Hodge dual \(G^{‚ô≠‚ô≠}\) of the Faraday 2-form \(F^{‚ô≠‚ô≠}\) is:

\[G^{‚ô≠‚ô≠} = ‚ãÜ F^{‚ô≠‚ô≠}\]

Expanded, we obtain:

\[\begin{split}G^{‚ô≠‚ô≠} = \left[ \begin{alignedat}{1} B^x \; & dt ‚àß dx \\ B^y \; & dt ‚àß dy \\ B^z \; & dt ‚àß dz \\ \E^x \; & dy ‚àß dz \\ \E^y \; & dz ‚àß dx \\ \E^z \; & dx ‚àß dy \\ \end{alignedat} \right]\end{split}\]

Calculations

Start from the Faraday 2-form

\[\begin{split}F^{‚ô≠‚ô≠} = \left[ \begin{aligned} - & \E^x \; dt ‚àß dx \\ - & \E^y \; dt ‚àß dy \\ - & \E^z \; dt ‚àß dz \\ & B^x \; dy ‚àß dz \\ & B^y \; dz ‚àß dx \\ & B^z \; dx ‚àß dy \\ \end{aligned} \right]\end{split}\]

Take the Hodge dual

\[\begin{split}G^{‚ô≠‚ô≠} = ‚ãÜ F^{‚ô≠‚ô≠} = ‚ãÜ \left[ \begin{aligned} - & \E^x \; dt ‚àß dx \\ - & \E^y \; dt ‚àß dy \\ - & \E^z \; dt ‚àß dz \\ & B^x \; dy ‚àß dz \\ & B^y \; dz ‚àß dx \\ & B^z \; dx ‚àß dy \\ \end{aligned} \right]\end{split}\]

Distribute the Hodge dual operator

\[\begin{split}G^{‚ô≠‚ô≠} = \left[ \begin{aligned} - & \E^x \; ‚ãÜ dt ‚àß dx \\ - & \E^y \; ‚ãÜ dt ‚àß dy \\ - & \E^z \; ‚ãÜ dt ‚àß dz \\ & B^x \; ‚ãÜ dy ‚àß dz \\ & B^y \; ‚ãÜ dz ‚àß dx \\ & B^z \; ‚ãÜ dx ‚àß dy \\ \end{aligned} \right]\end{split}\]

Apply the Hodge dual operator

You can find the Hodge dual of each bivector basis in Minkowski space here.

\[\begin{split}G^{‚ô≠‚ô≠} = \left[ \begin{alignedat}{2} - & \E^x \; (-1) & dy ‚àß dz \\ - & \E^y \; (-1) & dz ‚àß dx \\ - & \E^z \; (-1) & dx ‚àß dy \\ & B^x \; (+1) & dt ‚àß dx \\ & B^y \; (+1) & dt ‚àß dy \\ & B^z \; (+1) & dt ‚àß dz \\ \end{alignedat} \right]\end{split}\]

Simplify

\[\begin{split}G^{‚ô≠‚ô≠} = \left[ \begin{alignedat}{1} \E^x \; & dy ‚àß dz \\ \E^y \; & dz ‚àß dx \\ \E^z \; & dx ‚àß dy \\ B^x \; & dt ‚àß dx \\ B^y \; & dt ‚àß dy \\ B^z \; & dt ‚àß dz \\ \end{alignedat} \right]\end{split}\]

Reorder

\[\begin{split}G^{‚ô≠‚ô≠} = \left[ \begin{alignedat}{1} B^x \; & dt ‚àß dx \\ B^y \; & dt ‚àß dy \\ B^z \; & dt ‚àß dz \\ \E^x \; & dy ‚àß dz \\ \E^y \; & dz ‚àß dx \\ \E^z \; & dx ‚àß dy \\ \end{alignedat} \right]\end{split}\]

From the dual Faraday 2-form and noting that 2-forms are tensors, we can derive the row/column representation of the doubly covariant dual Faraday tensor.

\[\begin{split}G^{‚ô≠‚ô≠} = \begin{bmatrix} & - B^x \; dx ‚àß dt & - B^y \; dy ‚àß dt & - B^z \; dz ‚àß dt \\ + B^x \; dt ‚àß dx & & - \E^z \; dy ‚àß dx & + \E^y \; dz ‚àß dx \\ + B^y \; dt ‚àß dy & + \E^z \; dx ‚àß dy & & - \E^x \; dz ‚àß dy \\ + B^z \; dt ‚àß dz & - \E^y \; dx ‚àß dz & + \E^x \; dy ‚àß dz & \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Calculations

Begin with the Hodge dual in column form

\[\begin{split}G^{‚ô≠‚ô≠} = \begin{bmatrix} B^x \; dt ‚àß dx \\ B^y \; dt ‚àß dy \\ B^z \; dt ‚àß dz \\ \E^x \; dy ‚àß dz \\ \E^y \; dz ‚àß dx \\ \E^z \; dx ‚àß dy \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Duplicate each 2-form

I am; indeed; really writing that \(A = \frac{1}{2} (A+A)\).

\[\begin{split}G^{‚ô≠‚ô≠} = \frac{1}{2} \left[ \begin{alignedat}{2} B^x \; dt ‚àß dx & \, + & B^x \; dt ‚àß dx \\ B^y \; dt ‚àß dy & \, + & B^y \; dt ‚àß dy \\ B^z \; dt ‚àß dz & \, + & B^z \; dt ‚àß dz \\ \E^x \; dy ‚àß dz & \, + & \E^x \; dy ‚àß dz \\ \E^y \; dz ‚àß dx & \, + & \E^y \; dz ‚àß dx \\ \E^z \; dx ‚àß dy & \, + & \E^z \; dx ‚àß dy \\ \end{alignedat} \right]\end{split}\]

Flip the exterior product

The purpose of the above operation was to utilize the antisymmetry of the exterior product and flip the signs \(dx^Œº ‚àß dx^ŒΩ = -dx^ŒΩ ‚àß dx^Œº\) as needed.

\[\begin{split}G^{‚ô≠‚ô≠} = \frac{1}{2} \left[ \begin{alignedat}{2} B^x \; dt ‚àß dx & \, - & B^x \; dx ‚àß dt \\ B^y \; dt ‚àß dy & \, - & B^y \; dy ‚àß dt \\ B^z \; dt ‚àß dz & \, - & B^z \; dz ‚àß dt \\ \E^x \; dy ‚àß dz & \, - & \E^x \; dz ‚àß dy \\ \E^y \; dz ‚àß dx & \, - & \E^y \; dx ‚àß dz \\ \E^z \; dx ‚àß dy & \, - & \E^z \; dy ‚àß dx \\ \end{alignedat} \right]\end{split}\]

We can now switch the representation of the dual Faraday 2-Form from a single row of basis 2-Forms, to a row/column representation.

Reorder into rows/column representation

From there, we conclude utilizing the free matrix representation of the Cartan-Hodge formalism, reordering the elements into rows and columns.

\[\begin{split}G^{‚ô≠‚ô≠} = \begin{bmatrix} & - B^x \; dx ‚àß dt & - B^y \; dy ‚àß dt & - B^z \; dz ‚àß dt \\ B^x \; dt ‚àß dx & & - \E^z \; dy ‚àß dx & + \E^y \; dz ‚àß dx \\ B^y \; dt ‚àß dy & + \E^z \; dx ‚àß dy & & - \E^x \; dz ‚àß dy \\ B^z \; dt ‚àß dz & - \E^y \; dx ‚àß dz & + \E^x \; dy ‚àß dz & \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]
