Rotations in 3 Dimensions#

by St├σphane Haussler

The Free Matrix Representation of Rotations#

The Free Matrix Representation is powerfull when using a bivector basis, since the elements of the matrix can be re-ordered at will. In three dimensions, rotations are possible on the three planes. A rotation is expressed as a linear combination of the three associated basis bivectors:

\[R = a \; έΙΓ_y έΙπ έΙΓ_z + b \; έΙΓ_z έΙπ έΙΓ_x + c \; έΙΓ_x έΙπ έΙΓ_y\]

With free matrix representation, the bivector can be written as a single column:

Representation of rotations as a single column of bivectors

\[\begin{split}R^{έβψέβψ} = \begin{bmatrix} a \; έΙΓ_y έΙπ έΙΓ_z \\ b \; έΙΓ_z έΙπ έΙΓ_x \\ c \; έΙΓ_x έΙπ έΙΓ_y \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Or alternatively with a row/column matrix notation:

\[\begin{split}R^{έβψέβψ} = \left[ \begin{alignedat}{1} & c \; έΙΓ_x έΙπ έΙΓ_y & \\ & & a \; έΙΓ_y έΙπ έΙΓ_z \\ b \; έΙΓ_z έΙπ έΙΓ_x & & \\ \end{alignedat} \right]\end{split}\]

The anti-symmetric property of the wedge product \(έΙΓ_i έΙπ έΙΓ_j = - έΙΓ_j έΙπ έΙΓ_i\) permit to split the terms:

\[έΙΓ_i έΙπ έΙΓ_j = \frac{1}{2} (έΙΓ_i έΙπ έΙΓ_j - έΙΓ_j έΙπ έΙΓ_i)\]

With a row/column representation, we obtain:

The matrix representation of rotations

\[\begin{split}R^{έβψέβψ} = \frac{1}{2} \begin{bmatrix} & - c \; έΙΓ_y έΙπ έΙΓ_x & + b \; έΙΓ_z έΙπ έΙΓ_x \\ + c \; έΙΓ_x έΙπ έΙΓ_y & & - a \; έΙΓ_z έΙπ έΙΓ_y \\ - b \; έΙΓ_x έΙπ έΙΓ_z & + a \; έΙΓ_y έΙπ έΙΓ_z & \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

The doubly contravariant rotation object under consideration exclusively operates on covectors. To fall back to matrix multiplication, we require a mixed tensor that takes vectors as input, necessating the flattening of one of the components.

\(έβφέβψ\) Representation#

Flattening the first index of of the doubly contravariant form of the rotation \(R\), we obtain:

\[\begin{split}R^{έβφέβψ} = \begin{bmatrix} & - c \; dy έΙπ έΙΓ_x & + b \; dz έΙπ έΙΓ_x \\ + c \; dx έΙπ έΙΓ_y & & - a \; dz έΙπ έΙΓ_y \\ - b \; dx έΙπ έΙΓ_z & + a \; dy έΙπ έΙΓ_z & \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Calculations

Flatten the first index, keeping the second sharp

\[R^{έβφέβψ} = (R^{έβψέβψ})^{έβφέβψ}\]

Take the rotation in its matrix form

\[\begin{split}R^{έβφέβψ} = \frac{1}{2} \begin{bmatrix} & - c \; έΙΓ_y έΙπ έΙΓ_x & + b \; έΙΓ_z έΙπ έΙΓ_x \\ + c \; έΙΓ_x έΙπ έΙΓ_y & & - a \; έΙΓ_z έΙπ έΙΓ_y \\ - b \; έΙΓ_x έΙπ έΙΓ_z & + a \; έΙΓ_y έΙπ έΙΓ_z & \\ \end{bmatrix}^{έβφέβψ}\end{split}\]

Distribute the musical operators

\[\begin{split}R^{έβφέβψ} = \frac{1}{2} \begin{bmatrix} & - c \; (έΙΓ_y έΙπ έΙΓ_x)^{έβφέβψ} & + b \; (έΙΓ_z έΙπ έΙΓ_x)^{έβφέβψ} \\ + c \; (έΙΓ_x έΙπ έΙΓ_y)^{έβφέβψ} & & - a \; (έΙΓ_z έΙπ έΙΓ_y)^{έβφέβψ} \\ - b \; (έΙΓ_x έΙπ έΙΓ_z)^{έβφέβψ} & + a \; (έΙΓ_y έΙπ έΙΓ_z)^{έβφέβψ} & \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Apply musical operators using the euclidean metric

\[\begin{split}R^{έβφέβψ} = \frac{1}{2} \begin{bmatrix} & - c \; ╬┤_{yi} dx^i έΙπ έΙΓ_x & + b \; ╬┤_{zi} dx^i έΙπ έΙΓ_x \\ + c \; ╬┤_{xi} dx^i έΙπ έΙΓ_y & & - a \; ╬┤_{zi} dx^i έΙπ έΙΓ_y \\ - b \; ╬┤_{xi} dx^i έΙπ έΙΓ_z & + a \; ╬┤_{yi} dx^i έΙπ έΙΓ_z & \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Identify the non-zero components

\[\begin{split}R^{έβφέβψ} = \frac{1}{2} \begin{bmatrix} & - c \; dx^y έΙπ έΙΓ_x & + b \; dx^z έΙπ έΙΓ_x \\ + c \; dx^x έΙπ έΙΓ_y & & - a \; dx^z έΙπ έΙΓ_y \\ - b \; dx^x έΙπ έΙΓ_z & + a \; dx^y έΙπ έΙΓ_z & \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Replace the covectors by their expressions and conclude

\[\begin{split}dx^x = dx \\ dx^y = dy \\ dx^z = dz \\\end{split}\]
\[\begin{split}R^{έβφέβψ} = \frac{1}{2} \begin{bmatrix} & - c \; dy έΙπ έΙΓ_x & + b \; dz έΙπ έΙΓ_x \\ + c \; dx έΙπ έΙΓ_y & & - a \; dz έΙπ έΙΓ_y \\ - b \; dx έΙπ έΙΓ_z & + a \; dy έΙπ έΙΓ_z & \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Expanding the wedge product to its tensor form and simplifying, we find the explicit expression of the mixed wedge products.

\[\begin{split}(έΙΓ_x έΙπ έΙΓ_y)^{έβφέβψ} &= dx έΛΩ έΙΓ_y - dy έΛΩ έΙΓ_x \\ (έΙΓ_y έΙπ έΙΓ_z)^{έβφέβψ} &= dy έΛΩ έΙΓ_z - dz έΛΩ έΙΓ_y \\ (έΙΓ_z έΙπ έΙΓ_x)^{έβφέβψ} &= dz έΛΩ έΙΓ_x - dx έΛΩ έΙΓ_z \\\end{split}\]

Calculations

Expand the wedge product into tensor product

\[\begin{split}(έΙΓ_x έΙπ έΙΓ_y)^{έβφέβψ} &= (έΙΓ_x έΛΩ έΙΓ_y - έΙΓ_y έΛΩ έΙΓ_x)^{έβφέβψ} \\ (έΙΓ_y έΙπ έΙΓ_z)^{έβφέβψ} &= (έΙΓ_y έΛΩ έΙΓ_z - έΙΓ_z έΛΩ έΙΓ_y)^{έβφέβψ} \\ (έΙΓ_z έΙπ έΙΓ_x)^{έβφέβψ} &= (έΙΓ_z έΛΩ έΙΓ_x - έΙΓ_x έΛΩ έΙΓ_z)^{έβφέβψ} \\\end{split}\]

Distribute the musical operators

\[\begin{split}(έΙΓ_x έΙπ έΙΓ_y)^{έβφέβψ} &= έΙΓ_x^έβφ έΛΩ έΙΓ_y^έβψ - έΙΓ_y^έβφ έΛΩ έΙΓ_x^έβψ \\ (έΙΓ_y έΙπ έΙΓ_z)^{έβφέβψ} &= έΙΓ_y^έβφ έΛΩ έΙΓ_z^έβψ - έΙΓ_z^έβφ έΛΩ έΙΓ_y^έβψ \\ (έΙΓ_z έΙπ έΙΓ_x)^{έβφέβψ} &= έΙΓ_z^έβφ έΛΩ έΙΓ_x^έβψ - έΙΓ_x^έβφ έΛΩ έΙΓ_z^έβψ \\\end{split}\]

Apply musical operators using the euclidean metric:

\[\begin{split}(έΙΓ_x έΙπ έΙΓ_y)^{έβφέβψ} &= ╬┤_{xi} dx^i έΛΩ έΙΓ_y - ╬┤_{yi} dx^i έΛΩ έΙΓ_x \\ (έΙΓ_y έΙπ έΙΓ_z)^{έβφέβψ} &= ╬┤_{yi} dx^i έΛΩ έΙΓ_z - ╬┤_{zi} dx^i έΛΩ έΙΓ_y \\ (έΙΓ_z έΙπ έΙΓ_x)^{έβφέβψ} &= ╬┤_{zi} dx^i έΛΩ έΙΓ_x - ╬┤_{xi} dx^i έΛΩ έΙΓ_z \\\end{split}\]

Identify the non-zero terms

\[\begin{split}(έΙΓ_x έΙπ έΙΓ_y)^{έβφέβψ} &= ╬┤_{xx} dx^x έΛΩ έΙΓ_y - ╬┤_{yy} dx^y έΛΩ έΙΓ_x \\ (έΙΓ_y έΙπ έΙΓ_z)^{έβφέβψ} &= ╬┤_{yy} dx^y έΛΩ έΙΓ_z - ╬┤_{zz} dx^z έΛΩ έΙΓ_y \\ (έΙΓ_z έΙπ έΙΓ_x)^{έβφέβψ} &= ╬┤_{zz} dx^z έΛΩ έΙΓ_x - ╬┤_{xx} dx^x έΛΩ έΙΓ_z \\\end{split}\]
\[\begin{split}(έΙΓ_x έΙπ έΙΓ_y)^{έβφέβψ} &= dx^x έΛΩ έΙΓ_y - dx^y έΛΩ έΙΓ_x \\ (έΙΓ_y έΙπ έΙΓ_z)^{έβφέβψ} &= dx^y έΛΩ έΙΓ_z - dx^z έΛΩ έΙΓ_y \\ (έΙΓ_z έΙπ έΙΓ_x)^{έβφέβψ} &= dx^z έΛΩ έΙΓ_x - dx^x έΛΩ έΙΓ_z \\\end{split}\]

Replace covectors by their expressions and conclude

\[\begin{split}dx^x = dx \\ dx^y = dy \\ dx^z = dz \\\end{split}\]
\[\begin{split}(έΙΓ_x έΙπ έΙΓ_y)^{έβφέβψ} &= dx έΛΩ έΙΓ_y - dy έΛΩ έΙΓ_x \\ (έΙΓ_y έΙπ έΙΓ_z)^{έβφέβψ} &= dy έΛΩ έΙΓ_z - dz έΛΩ έΙΓ_y \\ (έΙΓ_z έΙπ έΙΓ_x)^{έβφέβψ} &= dz έΛΩ έΙΓ_x - dx έΛΩ έΙΓ_z \\\end{split}\]

\(έβψέβφ\) Representation#

Flattening the second index of of the doubly contravariant form of the rotation \(R\), we obtain:

\[\begin{split}R^{έβψέβφ} = \frac{1}{2} \begin{bmatrix} & + c \; έΙΓ_x έΙπ dy & - b \; έΙΓ_x έΙπ dz \\ - c \; έΙΓ_y έΙπ dx & & + a \; έΙΓ_y έΙπ dz \\ + b \; έΙΓ_z έΙπ dx & - a \; έΙΓ_z έΙπ dy & \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Calculations

Flatten the second index, keeping the first sharp:

\[R^{έβψέβφ} = (R^{έβψέβψ})^{έβψέβφ}\]

Take the rotation in its matrix form:

\[\begin{split}R^{έβψέβφ} = \frac{1}{2} \begin{bmatrix} & - c \; έΙΓ_y έΙπ έΙΓ_x & + b \; έΙΓ_z έΙπ έΙΓ_x \\ + c \; έΙΓ_x έΙπ έΙΓ_y & & - a \; έΙΓ_z έΙπ έΙΓ_y \\ - b \; έΙΓ_x έΙπ έΙΓ_z & + a \; έΙΓ_y έΙπ έΙΓ_z & \\ \end{bmatrix}^{έβψέβφ}\end{split}\]

Distribute the musical operators:

\[\begin{split}R^{έβψέβφ} = \frac{1}{2} \begin{bmatrix} & - c \; (έΙΓ_y έΙπ έΙΓ_x)^{έβψέβφ} & + b \; (έΙΓ_z έΙπ έΙΓ_x)^{έβψέβφ} \\ + c \; (έΙΓ_x έΙπ έΙΓ_y)^{έβψέβφ} & & - a \; (έΙΓ_z έΙπ έΙΓ_y)^{έβψέβφ} \\ - b \; (έΙΓ_x έΙπ έΙΓ_z)^{έβψέβφ} & + a \; (έΙΓ_y έΙπ έΙΓ_z)^{έβψέβφ} & \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Apply the musical operators using the euclidean metric:

\[\begin{split}R^{έβψέβφ} = \frac{1}{2} \begin{bmatrix} & - c \; έΙΓ_y έΙπ ╬┤_{xi} dx^i & + b \; έΙΓ_z έΙπ ╬┤_{xi} dx^i \\ + c \; έΙΓ_x έΙπ ╬┤_{yi} dx^i & & - a \; έΙΓ_z έΙπ ╬┤_{yi} dx^i \\ - b \; έΙΓ_x έΙπ ╬┤_{zi} dx^i & + a \; έΙΓ_y έΙπ ╬┤_{zi} dx^i & \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Identify the non-zero components:

\[\begin{split}R^{έβψέβφ} = \frac{1}{2} \begin{bmatrix} & - c \; έΙΓ_y έΙπ dx^x & + b \; έΙΓ_z έΙπ dx^x \\ + c \; έΙΓ_x έΙπ dx^y & & - a \; έΙΓ_z έΙπ dx^y \\ - b \; έΙΓ_x έΙπ dx^z & + a \; έΙΓ_y έΙπ dx^z & \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Replace the covectors by their expressions

\[\begin{split}dx^x = dx \\ dx^y = dy \\ dx^z = dz \\\end{split}\]
\[\begin{split}R^{έβψέβφ} = \frac{1}{2} \begin{bmatrix} & - c \; έΙΓ_y έΙπ dx & + b \; έΙΓ_z έΙπ dx \\ + c \; έΙΓ_x έΙπ dy & & - a \; έΙΓ_z έΙπ dy \\ - b \; έΙΓ_x έΙπ dz & + a \; έΙΓ_y έΙπ dz & \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Reorder and conclude

\[\begin{split}R^{έβψέβφ} = \frac{1}{2} \begin{bmatrix} & + c \; έΙΓ_x έΙπ dy & - b \; έΙΓ_x έΙπ dz \\ - c \; έΙΓ_y έΙπ dx & & + a \; έΙΓ_y έΙπ dz \\ + b \; έΙΓ_z έΙπ dx & - a \; έΙΓ_z έΙπ dy & \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Expanding the wedge product to its tensor form and simplifying, we find the explicit expression of the mixed wedge products.

\[\begin{split}(έΙΓ_x έΙπ έΙΓ_y)^{έβψέβφ} &= έΙΓ_x έΛΩ dy - έΙΓ_y έΛΩ dx \\ (έΙΓ_y έΙπ έΙΓ_z)^{έβψέβφ} &= έΙΓ_y έΛΩ dz - έΙΓ_z έΛΩ dy \\ (έΙΓ_z έΙπ έΙΓ_x)^{έβψέβφ} &= έΙΓ_z έΛΩ dx - έΙΓ_x έΛΩ dz \\\end{split}\]

Calculations

Expand exterior products into tensor products

\[\begin{split}(έΙΓ_x έΙπ έΙΓ_y)^{έβψέβφ} &= (έΙΓ_x έΛΩ έΙΓ_y - έΙΓ_y έΛΩ έΙΓ_x)^{έβψέβφ} \\ (έΙΓ_y έΙπ έΙΓ_z)^{έβψέβφ} &= (έΙΓ_y έΛΩ έΙΓ_z - έΙΓ_z έΛΩ έΙΓ_y)^{έβψέβφ} \\ (έΙΓ_z έΙπ έΙΓ_x)^{έβψέβφ} &= (έΙΓ_z έΛΩ έΙΓ_x - έΙΓ_x έΛΩ έΙΓ_z)^{έβψέβφ} \\\end{split}\]

Distribute the musical operators

\[\begin{split}(έΙΓ_x έΙπ έΙΓ_y)^{έβψέβφ} &= έΙΓ_x^έβψ έΛΩ έΙΓ_y^έβφ - έΙΓ_y^έβψ έΛΩ έΙΓ_x^έβφ \\ (έΙΓ_y έΙπ έΙΓ_z)^{έβψέβφ} &= έΙΓ_y^έβψ έΛΩ έΙΓ_z^έβφ - έΙΓ_z^έβψ έΛΩ έΙΓ_y^έβφ \\ (έΙΓ_z έΙπ έΙΓ_x)^{έβψέβφ} &= έΙΓ_z^έβψ έΛΩ έΙΓ_x^έβφ - έΙΓ_x^έβψ έΛΩ έΙΓ_z^έβφ \\\end{split}\]

Apply the musical operators using the euclidean metric

\[\begin{split}(έΙΓ_x έΙπ έΙΓ_y)^{έβψέβφ} &= έΙΓ_x έΛΩ ╬┤_{yi} dx^i - έΙΓ_y έΛΩ ╬┤_{xi} dx^i \\ (έΙΓ_y έΙπ έΙΓ_z)^{έβψέβφ} &= έΙΓ_y έΛΩ ╬┤_{zi} dx^i - έΙΓ_z έΛΩ ╬┤_{yi} dx^i \\ (έΙΓ_z έΙπ έΙΓ_x)^{έβψέβφ} &= έΙΓ_z έΛΩ ╬┤_{xi} dx^i - έΙΓ_x έΛΩ ╬┤_{zi} dx^i \\\end{split}\]

Identify the non-zero terms

\[\begin{split}(έΙΓ_x έΙπ έΙΓ_y)^{έβψέβφ} &= έΙΓ_x έΛΩ ╬┤_{yy} dx^y - έΙΓ_y έΛΩ ╬┤_{xx} dx^x \\ (έΙΓ_y έΙπ έΙΓ_z)^{έβψέβφ} &= έΙΓ_y έΛΩ ╬┤_{zz} dx^z - έΙΓ_z έΛΩ ╬┤_{yy} dx^y \\ (έΙΓ_z έΙπ έΙΓ_x)^{έβψέβφ} &= έΙΓ_z έΛΩ ╬┤_{xx} dx^x - έΙΓ_x έΛΩ ╬┤_{zz} dx^z \\\end{split}\]

Apply numerical values

\[\begin{split}(έΙΓ_x έΙπ έΙΓ_y)^{έβψέβφ} &= έΙΓ_x έΛΩ dx^y - έΙΓ_y έΛΩ dx^x \\ (έΙΓ_y έΙπ έΙΓ_z)^{έβψέβφ} &= έΙΓ_y έΛΩ dx^z - έΙΓ_z έΛΩ dx^y \\ (έΙΓ_z έΙπ έΙΓ_x)^{έβψέβφ} &= έΙΓ_z έΛΩ dx^x - έΙΓ_x έΛΩ dx^z \\\end{split}\]

Replace the covectors by their expressions:

\[\begin{split}dx^x = dx \\ dx^y = dy \\ dx^z = dz \\\end{split}\]
\[\begin{split}(έΙΓ_x έΙπ έΙΓ_y)^{έβψέβφ} &= έΙΓ_x έΛΩ dy - έΙΓ_y έΛΩ dx \\ (έΙΓ_y έΙπ έΙΓ_z)^{έβψέβφ} &= έΙΓ_y έΛΩ dz - έΙΓ_z έΛΩ dy \\ (έΙΓ_z έΙπ έΙΓ_x)^{έβψέβφ} &= έΙΓ_z έΛΩ dx - έΙΓ_x έΛΩ dz \\\end{split}\]

The \(\mathfrak{so}(3)\) Rotation Group#

Whether as a transpose or not, we identify the \(\mathfrak{so}(3)\) matrices as well as get a first hint that we are about to identify the electromagnetic tensor. Choosing the implicit basis \(\mathbf{e}_i \wedge \mathbf{e}_j\) in a row major representation, we obtain:

\begin{align} R &= \frac{1}{2} \begin{bmatrix} & - c & + b \\ + c & & - a \\ - b & + a & \\ \end{bmatrix} \\ &= a \left[ \begin{alignedat}{1} \; 0 & \; & 0 & \; & 0 \\ \; 0 & \; & 0 & \;- & 1 \\ \; 0 & \; + & 1 & \; & 0 \\ \end{alignedat} \right] + b \left[ \begin{alignedat}{1} & 0 & \quad 0 & \; + & 1 \\ & 0 & \quad 0 & \; & 0 \\ - & 1 & \quad 0 & \; & 0 \\ \end{alignedat} \right] + c \left[ \begin{alignedat}{1} & 0 & - & 1 & \quad 0 \\ + & 1 & & 0 & \quad 0 \\ & 0 & & 0 & \quad 0 \\ \end{alignedat} \right] \end{align}

Which is a regular choice for the basis of the \(\mathfrak{so}(3)\) group.

The Cross Product#

Rotations in three dimensions have a dual. We can either express a rotation along the three planes, or we can express a rotation along the three directions of space. Indeed, through the use of the Hodge star \(έΜΗ\), we fall back to the description of rotations expressed as a cross product \(έρψ\):

Apply the Hodge star:

\[έΜΗR = έΜΗ(a \; έΙΓ_y έΙπ έΙΓ_z + b \; έΙΓ_z έΙπ έΙΓ_x + c \; έΙΓ_x έΙπ έΙΓ_y)\]

Distribute the Hodge star:

\[έΜΗR = a έΜΗ(έΙΓ_y έΙπ έΙΓ_z) + b έΜΗ(έΙΓ_z έΙπ έΙΓ_x) + c έΜΗ(έΙΓ_x έΙπ έΙΓ_y)\]

Identify the cross product:

\[έΜΗR = a \; έΙΓ_x + b \; έΙΓ_y + c \; έΙΓ_z\]

That is, the Hodge star of the rotation έΙΓ_xpressed as a linear comibination of bivectors is exactly a rotation in terms of cross products in the Hodge dual space:

\[έΜΗR = a \; έΙΓ_y έρψ έΙΓ_z + b \; έΙΓ_z έρψ έΙΓ_x + c \; έΙΓ_x έρψ έΙΓ_y\]

We could have written a covector in the same explicit manner. This notation is very conveniant when performing calculations in CartanέΑβs framework as it permits to identify and organize terms for practical calculations by falling back to regular matrix multiplication.
