Rotations in 3 Dimensions#

by St√©phane Haussler

On this page, I systematically explore the representation of infinitesimal rotations in 3-dimensional Euclidean space using the language of differential forms. We establish the relation to matrix multiplication rules, as well as the direct relation to the cross product. In preparation for the analysis of rotations in 4-dimensional Minkowski spacetime, we determine the symmetries of the exterior product in mixed form, which, in this case, are found to be trivial.

The Free Matrix Representation of Rotations#

The Free Matrix Representation is intuitive when using a bivector basis, since the elements can be organized and re-ordered at will. With three dimensions, rotations are possible on the three planes and can be expressed as a linear combinations the three basis bivectors:

\[R^{‚ôĮ‚ôĮ} = a \; ‚ąā_y ‚ąß ‚ąā_z + b \; ‚ąā_z ‚ąß ‚ąā_x + c \; ‚ąā_x ‚ąß ‚ąā_y\]

We can rewrite as a single column:

Representation of rotations as a single column of bivectors

\[\begin{split}R^{‚ôĮ‚ôĮ} = \begin{bmatrix} a \; ‚ąā_y ‚ąß ‚ąā_z \\ b \; ‚ąā_z ‚ąß ‚ąā_x \\ c \; ‚ąā_x ‚ąß ‚ąā_y \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

We could also represent the rotation with a row/column notation:

\[\begin{split}R^{‚ôĮ‚ôĮ} = \left[ \begin{alignedat}{1} & c \; ‚ąā_x ‚ąß ‚ąā_y & \\ & & a \; ‚ąā_y ‚ąß ‚ąā_z \\ b \; ‚ąā_z ‚ąß ‚ąā_x & & \\ \end{alignedat} \right]\end{split}\]

However there is a more natural representation. The exterior product is anti-symmetric \(‚ąā_i ‚ąß ‚ąā_j = - ‚ąā_j ‚ąß ‚ąā_i\) and strictly equivalent to \(‚ąā_i ‚ąß ‚ąā_j = \frac{1}{2} (‚ąā_i ‚ąß ‚ąā_j - ‚ąā_j ‚ąß ‚ąā_i)\), which permits to rewrite:

\[\begin{split}R^{‚ôĮ‚ôĮ} = \begin{bmatrix} a \; ‚ąā_y ‚ąß ‚ąā_z \\ b \; ‚ąā_z ‚ąß ‚ąā_x \\ c \; ‚ąā_x ‚ąß ‚ąā_y \\ \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} a \; ‚ąā_y ‚ąß ‚ąā_z - a \; ‚ąā_z ‚ąß ‚ąā_y \\ b \; ‚ąā_z ‚ąß ‚ąā_x - b \; ‚ąā_x ‚ąß ‚ąā_z \\ c \; ‚ąā_x ‚ąß ‚ąā_y - c \; ‚ąā_y ‚ąß ‚ąā_x \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

With a row/column representation, we obtain a fully anti-symmetric and doubly contravariant representation:

The doubly contravariant row/column representation of rotations

\[\begin{split}R^{‚ôĮ‚ôĮ} = \frac{1}{2} \begin{bmatrix} & - c \; ‚ąā_y ‚ąß ‚ąā_x & + b \; ‚ąā_z ‚ąß ‚ąā_x \\ + c \; ‚ąā_x ‚ąß ‚ąā_y & & - a \; ‚ąā_z ‚ąß ‚ąā_y \\ - b \; ‚ąā_x ‚ąß ‚ąā_z & + a \; ‚ąā_y ‚ąß ‚ąā_z & \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

The doubly contravariant rotation obtained exclusively operates on covectors. Falling back to matrix multiplication rules requires a mixed tensor that takes a vector as input, and output a vector as output. Specifically, we need to flatten the first component in oder to obtain the \(‚ôĮ‚ô≠\) tensor representation, which corresponds exactly to the matrix representation commonly encountered in linear algebra.

\(‚ô≠‚ôĮ\) Representation#

Flattening the first index of of the doubly contravariant form of the rotation \(R\), we obtain:

\[\begin{split}R^{‚ô≠‚ôĮ} = \begin{bmatrix} & - c \; dy ‚ąß ‚ąā_x & + b \; dz ‚ąß ‚ąā_x \\ + c \; dx ‚ąß ‚ąā_y & & - a \; dz ‚ąß ‚ąā_y \\ - b \; dx ‚ąß ‚ąā_z & + a \; dy ‚ąß ‚ąā_z & \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Calculations

Flatten the first index, keeping the second sharp

\[R^{‚ô≠‚ôĮ} = (R^{‚ôĮ‚ôĮ})^{‚ô≠‚ôĮ}\]

Take the doubly contravariant row/column representation

\[\begin{split}R^{‚ô≠‚ôĮ} = \frac{1}{2} \begin{bmatrix} & - c \; ‚ąā_y ‚ąß ‚ąā_x & + b \; ‚ąā_z ‚ąß ‚ąā_x \\ + c \; ‚ąā_x ‚ąß ‚ąā_y & & - a \; ‚ąā_z ‚ąß ‚ąā_y \\ - b \; ‚ąā_x ‚ąß ‚ąā_z & + a \; ‚ąā_y ‚ąß ‚ąā_z & \\ \end{bmatrix}^{‚ô≠‚ôĮ}\end{split}\]

Distribute the musical operators

\[\begin{split}R^{‚ô≠‚ôĮ} = \frac{1}{2} \begin{bmatrix} & - c \; (‚ąā_y ‚ąß ‚ąā_x)^{‚ô≠‚ôĮ} & + b \; (‚ąā_z ‚ąß ‚ąā_x)^{‚ô≠‚ôĮ} \\ + c \; (‚ąā_x ‚ąß ‚ąā_y)^{‚ô≠‚ôĮ} & & - a \; (‚ąā_z ‚ąß ‚ąā_y)^{‚ô≠‚ôĮ} \\ - b \; (‚ąā_x ‚ąß ‚ąā_z)^{‚ô≠‚ôĮ} & + a \; (‚ąā_y ‚ąß ‚ąā_z)^{‚ô≠‚ôĮ} & \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Apply the musical operators, using the euclidean metric

\[\begin{split}R^{‚ô≠‚ôĮ} = \frac{1}{2} \begin{bmatrix} & - c \; őī_{yi} dx^i ‚ąß ‚ąā_x & + b \; őī_{zi} dx^i ‚ąß ‚ąā_x \\ + c \; őī_{xi} dx^i ‚ąß ‚ąā_y & & - a \; őī_{zi} dx^i ‚ąß ‚ąā_y \\ - b \; őī_{xi} dx^i ‚ąß ‚ąā_z & + a \; őī_{yi} dx^i ‚ąß ‚ąā_z & \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Identify the non-zero components and conclude

\[\begin{split}R^{‚ô≠‚ôĮ} = \frac{1}{2} \begin{bmatrix} & - c \; dy ‚ąß ‚ąā_x & + b \; dz ‚ąß ‚ąā_x \\ + c \; dx ‚ąß ‚ąā_y & & - a \; dz ‚ąß ‚ąā_y \\ - b \; dx ‚ąß ‚ąā_z & + a \; dy ‚ąß ‚ąā_z & \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Expanding the exterior product to its tensor form and simplifying, we find the explicit expression in terms of tensor products \(‚äó\):

\[\begin{split}(‚ąā_x ‚ąß ‚ąā_y)^{‚ô≠‚ôĮ} &= dx ‚äó ‚ąā_y - dy ‚äó ‚ąā_x \\ (‚ąā_y ‚ąß ‚ąā_z)^{‚ô≠‚ôĮ} &= dy ‚äó ‚ąā_z - dz ‚äó ‚ąā_y \\ (‚ąā_z ‚ąß ‚ąā_x)^{‚ô≠‚ôĮ} &= dz ‚äó ‚ąā_x - dx ‚äó ‚ąā_z \\\end{split}\]

Calculations

Expand the exterior product into tensor products

\[\begin{split}(‚ąā_x ‚ąß ‚ąā_y)^{‚ô≠‚ôĮ} &= (‚ąā_x ‚äó ‚ąā_y - ‚ąā_y ‚äó ‚ąā_x)^{‚ô≠‚ôĮ} \\ (‚ąā_y ‚ąß ‚ąā_z)^{‚ô≠‚ôĮ} &= (‚ąā_y ‚äó ‚ąā_z - ‚ąā_z ‚äó ‚ąā_y)^{‚ô≠‚ôĮ} \\ (‚ąā_z ‚ąß ‚ąā_x)^{‚ô≠‚ôĮ} &= (‚ąā_z ‚äó ‚ąā_x - ‚ąā_x ‚äó ‚ąā_z)^{‚ô≠‚ôĮ} \\\end{split}\]

Distribute the musical operators

\[\begin{split}(‚ąā_x ‚ąß ‚ąā_y)^{‚ô≠‚ôĮ} &= ‚ąā_x^‚ô≠ ‚äó ‚ąā_y^‚ôĮ - ‚ąā_y^‚ô≠ ‚äó ‚ąā_x^‚ôĮ \\ (‚ąā_y ‚ąß ‚ąā_z)^{‚ô≠‚ôĮ} &= ‚ąā_y^‚ô≠ ‚äó ‚ąā_z^‚ôĮ - ‚ąā_z^‚ô≠ ‚äó ‚ąā_y^‚ôĮ \\ (‚ąā_z ‚ąß ‚ąā_x)^{‚ô≠‚ôĮ} &= ‚ąā_z^‚ô≠ ‚äó ‚ąā_x^‚ôĮ - ‚ąā_x^‚ô≠ ‚äó ‚ąā_z^‚ôĮ \\\end{split}\]

Apply musical operators using the Euclidean metric:

\[\begin{split}(‚ąā_x ‚ąß ‚ąā_y)^{‚ô≠‚ôĮ} &= őī_{xi} dx^i ‚äó ‚ąā_y - őī_{yi} dx^i ‚äó ‚ąā_x \\ (‚ąā_y ‚ąß ‚ąā_z)^{‚ô≠‚ôĮ} &= őī_{yi} dx^i ‚äó ‚ąā_z - őī_{zi} dx^i ‚äó ‚ąā_y \\ (‚ąā_z ‚ąß ‚ąā_x)^{‚ô≠‚ôĮ} &= őī_{zi} dx^i ‚äó ‚ąā_x - őī_{xi} dx^i ‚äó ‚ąā_z \\\end{split}\]

Identify the non-zero terms

\[\begin{split}(‚ąā_x ‚ąß ‚ąā_y)^{‚ô≠‚ôĮ} &= őī_{xx} dx ‚äó ‚ąā_y - őī_{yy} dy ‚äó ‚ąā_x \\ (‚ąā_y ‚ąß ‚ąā_z)^{‚ô≠‚ôĮ} &= őī_{yy} dy ‚äó ‚ąā_z - őī_{zz} dz ‚äó ‚ąā_y \\ (‚ąā_z ‚ąß ‚ąā_x)^{‚ô≠‚ôĮ} &= őī_{zz} dz ‚äó ‚ąā_x - őī_{xx} dx ‚äó ‚ąā_z \\\end{split}\]

Conclude

\[\begin{split}(‚ąā_x ‚ąß ‚ąā_y)^{‚ô≠‚ôĮ} &= dx ‚äó ‚ąā_y - dy ‚äó ‚ąā_x \\ (‚ąā_y ‚ąß ‚ąā_z)^{‚ô≠‚ôĮ} &= dy ‚äó ‚ąā_z - dz ‚äó ‚ąā_y \\ (‚ąā_z ‚ąß ‚ąā_x)^{‚ô≠‚ôĮ} &= dz ‚äó ‚ąā_x - dx ‚äó ‚ąā_z \\\end{split}\]

\(‚ôĮ‚ô≠\) Representation#

Flattening the second index of of the doubly contravariant form of the rotation \(R\), we obtain:

\[\begin{split}R^{‚ôĮ‚ô≠} = \frac{1}{2} \begin{bmatrix} & + c \; ‚ąā_x ‚ąß dy & - b \; ‚ąā_x ‚ąß dz \\ - c \; ‚ąā_y ‚ąß dx & & + a \; ‚ąā_y ‚ąß dz \\ + b \; ‚ąā_z ‚ąß dx & - a \; ‚ąā_z ‚ąß dy & \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Calculations

Flatten the second index, keeping the first sharp:

\[R^{‚ôĮ‚ô≠} = (R^{‚ôĮ‚ôĮ})^{‚ôĮ‚ô≠}\]

Take the rotation in its matrix form:

\[\begin{split}R^{‚ôĮ‚ô≠} = \frac{1}{2} \begin{bmatrix} & - c \; ‚ąā_y ‚ąß ‚ąā_x & + b \; ‚ąā_z ‚ąß ‚ąā_x \\ + c \; ‚ąā_x ‚ąß ‚ąā_y & & - a \; ‚ąā_z ‚ąß ‚ąā_y \\ - b \; ‚ąā_x ‚ąß ‚ąā_z & + a \; ‚ąā_y ‚ąß ‚ąā_z & \\ \end{bmatrix}^{‚ôĮ‚ô≠}\end{split}\]

Distribute the musical operators:

\[\begin{split}R^{‚ôĮ‚ô≠} = \frac{1}{2} \begin{bmatrix} & - c \; (‚ąā_y ‚ąß ‚ąā_x)^{‚ôĮ‚ô≠} & + b \; (‚ąā_z ‚ąß ‚ąā_x)^{‚ôĮ‚ô≠} \\ + c \; (‚ąā_x ‚ąß ‚ąā_y)^{‚ôĮ‚ô≠} & & - a \; (‚ąā_z ‚ąß ‚ąā_y)^{‚ôĮ‚ô≠} \\ - b \; (‚ąā_x ‚ąß ‚ąā_z)^{‚ôĮ‚ô≠} & + a \; (‚ąā_y ‚ąß ‚ąā_z)^{‚ôĮ‚ô≠} & \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Apply the musical operators using the euclidean metric:

\[\begin{split}R^{‚ôĮ‚ô≠} = \frac{1}{2} \begin{bmatrix} & - c \; ‚ąā_y ‚ąß őī_{xi} dx^i & + b \; ‚ąā_z ‚ąß őī_{xi} dx^i \\ + c \; ‚ąā_x ‚ąß őī_{yi} dx^i & & - a \; ‚ąā_z ‚ąß őī_{yi} dx^i \\ - b \; ‚ąā_x ‚ąß őī_{zi} dx^i & + a \; ‚ąā_y ‚ąß őī_{zi} dx^i & \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Identify the non-zero components:

\[\begin{split}R^{‚ôĮ‚ô≠} = \frac{1}{2} \begin{bmatrix} & - c \; ‚ąā_y ‚ąß dx^x & + b \; ‚ąā_z ‚ąß dx^x \\ + c \; ‚ąā_x ‚ąß dx^y & & - a \; ‚ąā_z ‚ąß dx^y \\ - b \; ‚ąā_x ‚ąß dx^z & + a \; ‚ąā_y ‚ąß dx^z & \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Replace the covectors by their expressions

\[\begin{split}dx^x = dx \\ dx^y = dy \\ dx^z = dz \\\end{split}\]
\[\begin{split}R^{‚ôĮ‚ô≠} = \frac{1}{2} \begin{bmatrix} & - c \; ‚ąā_y ‚ąß dx & + b \; ‚ąā_z ‚ąß dx \\ + c \; ‚ąā_x ‚ąß dy & & - a \; ‚ąā_z ‚ąß dy \\ - b \; ‚ąā_x ‚ąß dz & + a \; ‚ąā_y ‚ąß dz & \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Reorder and conclude

\[\begin{split}R^{‚ôĮ‚ô≠} = \frac{1}{2} \begin{bmatrix} & + c \; ‚ąā_x ‚ąß dy & - b \; ‚ąā_x ‚ąß dz \\ - c \; ‚ąā_y ‚ąß dx & & + a \; ‚ąā_y ‚ąß dz \\ + b \; ‚ąā_z ‚ąß dx & - a \; ‚ąā_z ‚ąß dy & \\ \end{bmatrix}\end{split}\]

Expanding the wedge product to its tensor form and simplifying, we find the explicit expression of the mixed wedge products.

\[\begin{split}(‚ąā_x ‚ąß ‚ąā_y)^{‚ôĮ‚ô≠} &= ‚ąā_x ‚äó dy - ‚ąā_y ‚äó dx \\ (‚ąā_y ‚ąß ‚ąā_z)^{‚ôĮ‚ô≠} &= ‚ąā_y ‚äó dz - ‚ąā_z ‚äó dy \\ (‚ąā_z ‚ąß ‚ąā_x)^{‚ôĮ‚ô≠} &= ‚ąā_z ‚äó dx - ‚ąā_x ‚äó dz \\\end{split}\]

Calculations

Expand exterior products into tensor products

\[\begin{split}(‚ąā_x ‚ąß ‚ąā_y)^{‚ôĮ‚ô≠} &= (‚ąā_x ‚äó ‚ąā_y - ‚ąā_y ‚äó ‚ąā_x)^{‚ôĮ‚ô≠} \\ (‚ąā_y ‚ąß ‚ąā_z)^{‚ôĮ‚ô≠} &= (‚ąā_y ‚äó ‚ąā_z - ‚ąā_z ‚äó ‚ąā_y)^{‚ôĮ‚ô≠} \\ (‚ąā_z ‚ąß ‚ąā_x)^{‚ôĮ‚ô≠} &= (‚ąā_z ‚äó ‚ąā_x - ‚ąā_x ‚äó ‚ąā_z)^{‚ôĮ‚ô≠} \\\end{split}\]

Distribute the musical operators

\[\begin{split}(‚ąā_x ‚ąß ‚ąā_y)^{‚ôĮ‚ô≠} &= ‚ąā_x^‚ôĮ ‚äó ‚ąā_y^‚ô≠ - ‚ąā_y^‚ôĮ ‚äó ‚ąā_x^‚ô≠ \\ (‚ąā_y ‚ąß ‚ąā_z)^{‚ôĮ‚ô≠} &= ‚ąā_y^‚ôĮ ‚äó ‚ąā_z^‚ô≠ - ‚ąā_z^‚ôĮ ‚äó ‚ąā_y^‚ô≠ \\ (‚ąā_z ‚ąß ‚ąā_x)^{‚ôĮ‚ô≠} &= ‚ąā_z^‚ôĮ ‚äó ‚ąā_x^‚ô≠ - ‚ąā_x^‚ôĮ ‚äó ‚ąā_z^‚ô≠ \\\end{split}\]

Apply the musical operators using the euclidean metric

\[\begin{split}(‚ąā_x ‚ąß ‚ąā_y)^{‚ôĮ‚ô≠} &= ‚ąā_x ‚äó őī_{yi} dx^i - ‚ąā_y ‚äó őī_{xi} dx^i \\ (‚ąā_y ‚ąß ‚ąā_z)^{‚ôĮ‚ô≠} &= ‚ąā_y ‚äó őī_{zi} dx^i - ‚ąā_z ‚äó őī_{yi} dx^i \\ (‚ąā_z ‚ąß ‚ąā_x)^{‚ôĮ‚ô≠} &= ‚ąā_z ‚äó őī_{xi} dx^i - ‚ąā_x ‚äó őī_{zi} dx^i \\\end{split}\]

Identify the non-zero terms

\[\begin{split}(‚ąā_x ‚ąß ‚ąā_y)^{‚ôĮ‚ô≠} &= ‚ąā_x ‚äó őī_{yy} dx^y - ‚ąā_y ‚äó őī_{xx} dx^x \\ (‚ąā_y ‚ąß ‚ąā_z)^{‚ôĮ‚ô≠} &= ‚ąā_y ‚äó őī_{zz} dx^z - ‚ąā_z ‚äó őī_{yy} dx^y \\ (‚ąā_z ‚ąß ‚ąā_x)^{‚ôĮ‚ô≠} &= ‚ąā_z ‚äó őī_{xx} dx^x - ‚ąā_x ‚äó őī_{zz} dx^z \\\end{split}\]

Apply numerical values

\[\begin{split}(‚ąā_x ‚ąß ‚ąā_y)^{‚ôĮ‚ô≠} &= ‚ąā_x ‚äó dx^y - ‚ąā_y ‚äó dx^x \\ (‚ąā_y ‚ąß ‚ąā_z)^{‚ôĮ‚ô≠} &= ‚ąā_y ‚äó dx^z - ‚ąā_z ‚äó dx^y \\ (‚ąā_z ‚ąß ‚ąā_x)^{‚ôĮ‚ô≠} &= ‚ąā_z ‚äó dx^x - ‚ąā_x ‚äó dx^z \\\end{split}\]

Replace the covectors by their expressions:

\[\begin{split}dx^x = dx \\ dx^y = dy \\ dx^z = dz \\\end{split}\]
\[\begin{split}(‚ąā_x ‚ąß ‚ąā_y)^{‚ôĮ‚ô≠} &= ‚ąā_x ‚äó dy - ‚ąā_y ‚äó dx \\ (‚ąā_y ‚ąß ‚ąā_z)^{‚ôĮ‚ô≠} &= ‚ąā_y ‚äó dz - ‚ąā_z ‚äó dy \\ (‚ąā_z ‚ąß ‚ąā_x)^{‚ôĮ‚ô≠} &= ‚ąā_z ‚äó dx - ‚ąā_x ‚äó dz \\\end{split}\]

The \(\mathfrak{so}(3)\) Rotation Group#

Whether as a transpose or not, we identify the \(\mathfrak{so}(3)\) matrices as well as get a first hint that we are about to identify the electromagnetic tensor. Choosing the implicit basis \(\mathbf{e}_i \wedge \mathbf{e}_j\) in a row major representation, we obtain:

\begin{align} R &= \frac{1}{2} \begin{bmatrix} & - c & + b \\ + c & & - a \\ - b & + a & \\ \end{bmatrix} \\ &= a \left[ \begin{alignedat}{1} \; 0 & \; & 0 & \; & 0 \\ \; 0 & \; & 0 & \;- & 1 \\ \; 0 & \; + & 1 & \; & 0 \\ \end{alignedat} \right] + b \left[ \begin{alignedat}{1} & 0 & \quad 0 & \; + & 1 \\ & 0 & \quad 0 & \; & 0 \\ - & 1 & \quad 0 & \; & 0 \\ \end{alignedat} \right] + c \left[ \begin{alignedat}{1} & 0 & - & 1 & \quad 0 \\ + & 1 & & 0 & \quad 0 \\ & 0 & & 0 & \quad 0 \\ \end{alignedat} \right] \end{align}

Which is a regular choice for the basis of the \(\mathfrak{so}(3)\) group.

The Cross Product#

Rotations in three dimensions have a dual. We can either express a rotation along the three planes, or we can express a rotation along the three directions of space. Indeed, through the use of the Hodge star \(‚čÜ\), we fall back to the description of rotations expressed as a cross product \(‚®Į\):

Apply the Hodge star:

\[‚čÜR = ‚čÜ(a \; ‚ąā_y ‚ąß ‚ąā_z + b \; ‚ąā_z ‚ąß ‚ąā_x + c \; ‚ąā_x ‚ąß ‚ąā_y)\]

Distribute the Hodge star:

\[‚čÜR = a ‚čÜ(‚ąā_y ‚ąß ‚ąā_z) + b ‚čÜ(‚ąā_z ‚ąß ‚ąā_x) + c ‚čÜ(‚ąā_x ‚ąß ‚ąā_y)\]

Identify the cross product:

\[‚čÜR = a \; ‚ąā_x + b \; ‚ąā_y + c \; ‚ąā_z\]

That is, the Hodge star of the rotation ‚ąā_xpressed as a linear comibination of bivectors is exactly a rotation in terms of cross products in the Hodge dual space:

\[‚čÜR = a \; ‚ąā_y ‚®Į ‚ąā_z + b \; ‚ąā_z ‚®Į ‚ąā_x + c \; ‚ąā_x ‚®Į ‚ąā_y\]

We could have written a covector in the same explicit manner. This notation is very conveniant when performing calculations in Cartan‚Äôs framework as it permits to identify and organize terms for practical calculations by falling back to regular matrix multiplication.
